GTA6 Pre-Orders Open Tomorrow — Everything You Need To Know

📅 June 24, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 9 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

Tomorrow is the day. After months of trailers, leaks, pricing speculation, and one very famous YouTube comment, Rockstar Games opens GTA6 pre-orders on June 25, 2026. The moment a significant portion of the gaming internet has been building toward for years is now less than 24 hours away.

But here's the thing: we still don't know the price. We don't know how many editions there are. We don't even know if Trailer 3 drops alongside the pre-order launch or a few hours before it. June 25 could be the most information-dense gaming day of the decade — or it could answer some questions and open twenty more.

This is your complete pre-orders eve guide. Everything confirmed, everything still unknown, and a clear-eyed breakdown of whether you should lock in a pre-order tomorrow the moment they go live — or take a breath and wait.

What Is Actually Confirmed Right Now

Rockstar and Take-Two have been unusually precise about the pre-order date. On June 18, the official cover art announcement made it explicit: pre-orders open June 25 across digital storefronts and major retailers. That's PS Store, Xbox Marketplace, and participating physical retail chains simultaneously.

The game itself launches November 19, 2026. That's a confirmed, locked, CEO-on-camera date. Strauss Zelnick confirmed it in a street interview, in earnings calls, and in every formal communication Take-Two has issued since the delay was walked back in early 2026. November 19 is not moving.

The platforms are PS5 and Xbox Series X|S at launch. No PC at launch — Rockstar has said nothing official about PC timing, and the PC community should plan around a 2027 window at the earliest based on GTA5's historical precedent.

✅ Confirmed

Pre-orders open June 25, 2026. Launch date November 19, 2026. Platforms: PS5 and Xbox Series X|S at launch. These three facts are locked, official, and not going anywhere.

The Price Problem — What We Still Don't Know

This is the biggest outstanding question going into tomorrow, and it's the one that will have the most immediate practical impact on your wallet. Rockstar has not confirmed a price. Take-Two has not confirmed a price. Not in earnings calls, not in investor presentations, not in the cover art announcement. Tomorrow's pre-order launch will be the first time we see the actual number.

What we do have is a trail of retailer signals. The Dutch Fnac listing that surfaced in early April showed a base price that translated to roughly $89.99 USD at current conversion rates. Italian retailer data from March pointed in a similar direction. Multiple analyst predictions — including notes from Wedbush Securities and MoffettNathanson — have pegged the standard edition at somewhere between $79.99 and $89.99, with the premium tier potentially pushing to $99.99 or beyond.

The logic for a price above $70 is real. GTA6 has been in development for over a decade. It represents the largest entertainment product ever created by any measure. Take-Two's balance sheet has been under pressure. And gaming's move toward $70 as the new standard — established with the PS5/Series X generation — has already been breached by several major titles. A $79.99 or $89.99 base price for GTA6 would be aggressive but not unprecedented in 2026.

⚠️ Heads Up

No official price has been confirmed for any GTA6 edition. Every number circulating right now — $79.99, $89.99, $99.99 — comes from retailer leaks, currency conversions, or analyst estimates. The actual price reveals tomorrow when pre-orders go live. Do not budget based on leaked figures alone.

The Edition Tier Question

Layered on top of the base price question is the editions question. A widely-circulated leak from April 2026 pointed to as many as six GTA6 editions at launch — ranging from a standard digital copy up through a collector's tier with physical extras. That leak has not been officially confirmed or denied.

What we can reasonably expect based on industry precedent: a standard edition, a deluxe or premium digital edition with bonus in-game content, and at least one physical collector's edition with packaging and extras. Whether Rockstar goes all the way to six distinct tiers — or whether that leak represented internal planning that got simplified — we won't know until tomorrow.

The practical advice here is to resist the urge to just grab the cheapest option in the heat of the moment. Take two minutes to read what's actually in each tier before you click buy. Bonus in-game currency, early access bonuses, and GTA Online starter packs have historically been bundled into premium editions — and some of those extras have had real value depending on how you play.

Trailer 3 — The Other Shoe That May Drop Tomorrow

Every signal points toward Trailer 3 dropping on June 25 alongside or immediately before the pre-order launch. The YouTube comment that surfaced under the official Rockstar Trailer 2 video — "consider us busy on June 25" — became one of the most-screenshotted pieces of gaming internet history this month. Whether that was a deliberate tease from Rockstar's social team or a low-level staffer getting creative, the community has treated it as near-confirmation.

The cadence supports it too. Trailer 1 dropped in December 2023 and sent the internet into complete frenzy. Trailer 2 came in May 2026 with the cover art announcement and the June 25 pre-order date. A three-trailer arc ending on pre-order day — the last marketing push before the campaign shifts to gameplay footage and launch week content — is exactly how major studios structure these campaigns.

ℹ️ Good To Know

Trailer 3 has not been officially announced for June 25. But the combination of the YouTube comment, the pre-order timing, and the standard three-trailer arc structure makes it the most anticipated unofficial event in gaming right now. Watch the Rockstar Games YouTube channel tomorrow.

What Trailer 3 Needs To Show

If Trailer 3 does drop tomorrow, it carries a very specific job. Trailer 1 introduced the world and Lucia. Trailer 2 showed Jason, established the tone, and confirmed Vice City as the setting with more detail than we'd ever seen. Trailer 3 needs to show gameplay. Not cinematic cutscenes, not stylised montages — actual mechanics. Open-world traversal. The driving model. Whether the gunplay feels like a generational leap. How the wanted system looks.

Players making a pre-order decision in real time need more than vibes. A two-to-three minute trailer that gives even a 30-second window into raw unscripted-feeling gameplay would dramatically accelerate pre-order momentum. Rockstar knows this. The question is whether they show it now or hold it for a dedicated gameplay reveal later in summer.

Community wishlist items beyond gameplay include: a confirmation of the Leonida Keys / Florida Keys open-water sailing mechanics that have been rumoured since the first trailer, more character time with Jason and Lucia's dual-protagonist dynamic, and — for the hardcore fans — anything that shows Vice City's interior spaces. Every apartment, office, and nightclub shown in trailers so far has been exterior. The city's interior life is still almost entirely unseen.

Should You Pre-Order Tomorrow — Or Wait?

This is the question that actually matters for most people reading this. And the honest answer is: it depends on what kind of player you are, and it depends on what the editions look like when they reveal tomorrow.

Here's the case for pre-ordering immediately. GTA6 is not a game where there's meaningful review embargo risk — Rockstar doesn't do early review copies, which removes the "wait for reviews" logic that applies to other titles. There's no scenario where GTA6 ships as a broken disaster on a major console in 2026. And if there's a Day 1 bonus or pre-order exclusive content tied to specific retailers, those windows often close. Physical pre-orders especially — if there's a specific collector's edition with limited production, the first 24 to 48 hours matter.

Here's the case for waiting. You don't know the price yet. You don't know the editions yet. Waiting 24 hours — until this article gets updated with confirmed details — costs you nothing unless a Day 1 exclusive closes. Digital pre-orders on PS Store and Xbox typically don't have supply limits. There's no queue for a digital code. You have time to read the edition breakdown before committing your money to a product you can't return.

What To Watch On Pre-Order Day

If you want to be on top of it the moment it happens, here's where to look. The Rockstar Games YouTube channel will be the source of truth for Trailer 3 if it drops. PlayStation Store and Xbox Marketplace will be the first digital storefronts with live pricing. GAME, GameStop, and major regional retailers will have physical edition details up on their product pages within minutes of the digital announcement.

Set a reminder. Not because pre-orders will sell out in seconds — digital pre-orders don't work that way — but because June 25 is going to be a firehose of information. Trailer 3 reactions, pricing discourse, edition comparisons, early impressions of the Day 1 bonuses, and the social media pile-on that follows any major gaming announcement. Having a clear checklist of what you actually care about before it starts will save you from making a decision in the chaos.

The Bigger Picture: Why June 25 Matters Beyond Pre-Orders

Step back from the pre-order mechanics for a moment. What's actually happening tomorrow is the official beginning of the GTA6 commercial campaign. Pre-orders opening means Rockstar and Take-Two are now selling the game to the public. From this point forward, the marketing machine runs at full power for five months straight leading into November 19.

The last time anything like this happened in gaming was GTA5 in 2013. And GTA5 went on to become the best-selling entertainment product in human history — a record it held for over a decade. GTA6 enters pre-order day as the most anticipated game ever made, by almost every metric you can measure: search volume, social listening data, analyst revenue projections, and the cultural weight of what this franchise means to hundreds of millions of players worldwide.

Tomorrow isn't just a transaction. It's the starting gun. The five-month build from June 25 to November 19 will include more gameplay reveals, the full edition breakdown, regional pricing announcements, platform-specific content deals, and — if Rockstar follows the GTA5 playbook — a dedicated GTA Online reveal that could be its own event. Every piece of that campaign starts tomorrow.

The Final Checklist For Tomorrow

Before you sleep tonight and wake up to pre-order day, here's everything to have in order:

  • Know your platform. PS5 or Xbox Series X|S. PC is not available at launch — don't wait on a PC pre-order that isn't coming.
  • Check your payment method. PS Store and Xbox accounts with expired cards or old billing info cause a surprising number of failed pre-orders on big release days.
  • Decide your budget ceiling in advance. Before you see the price, decide what you're willing to spend. This prevents the "fine, I'll just get the $129 edition" impulse buy when the listing goes live.
  • Watch Rockstar's YouTube first. If Trailer 3 drops, it drops there. Don't get the reaction video before you get the trailer itself.
  • Bookmark this article. We'll update with confirmed pricing, edition breakdowns, and Trailer 3 notes the moment they're live.
