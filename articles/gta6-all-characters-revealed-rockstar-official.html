GTA6 Characters Revealed — Every Person on Rockstar's Official Site, Explained

📅 June 25, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 10 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

Pre-order day just got a lot bigger. Alongside opening GTA6 pre-orders this morning, Rockstar Games updated the official GTA6 site at rockstargames.com with something the community has been waiting years for: the full, named, officially-described character roster. No more guessing at cover art faces. No more piecing together trailer background figures. Eight characters. Official bios. Direct from Rockstar.

This is the single most significant lore drop since Trailer 1. Every character listed on the official site is canon. The names, the descriptions, the connections between them — this is what Rockstar wants you to know before you pick up the game in November. And reading it carefully, there's a lot being said between the lines about what kind of story GTA6 is actually going to tell.

Here is every character, every official detail, and what it all means for the world of Vice City in 2026.

✅ Confirmed — Straight From Rockstar

Every character detail in this article comes directly from the official GTA6 site at rockstargames.com, updated on pre-order day June 25, 2026. No leaks. No speculation. This is the official word from Rockstar Games.

Jason — The Other Half of Vice City's Most Dangerous Partnership

Jason
Protagonist — Dual Lead

Jason is one of GTA6's two playable protagonists. Where Lucia arrived in Trailer 1 as the character the world latched onto — the focus, the energy, the one who looked directly at camera — Jason has emerged as her equal and counterpart in the official framing. He is not a sidekick. He is not the straight man. The official Rockstar description positions him as a man defined by loyalty and the complications that loyalty creates, operating in Vice City's criminal landscape alongside Lucia as a genuine partner.

What we've seen of Jason in the trailers is a man who is physically capable and emotionally present in ways that GTA protagonists haven't always been. The dual-protagonist structure — confirmed since Trailer 2 — means the narrative will weave between Jason and Lucia's perspectives, and the official site makes clear they are bound together not just circumstantially but by genuine connection. Their relationship is the engine of the story.

Jason represents something GTA hasn't done before at this scale: a male protagonist whose emotional arc is explicitly tied to a woman he views as an equal, not an objective. The Vice City that Jason and Lucia inhabit is one they are navigating together, making decisions together, and facing consequences together.

Lucia — The Face of GTA6, Now With Full Official Context

Lucia
Protagonist — Dual Lead

Lucia became the most talked-about character in gaming the moment she appeared in Trailer 1. The first female playable protagonist in mainline GTA history — that headline wrote itself. But the official site description goes beyond the historic framing and tells us something specific about who Lucia actually is as a character.

Rockstar's official bio describes Lucia as someone shaped by circumstances rather than purely by ambition — a woman trying to build something real out of an environment that keeps pulling her back toward chaos. Her Latina heritage and the Vice City setting interlock intentionally; this is not a character dropped into a generic backdrop but someone whose identity is part of the story's texture.

The cover art placed Lucia front and center for a reason. She is GTA6's primary narrative voice. Every story beat in the trailers has been framed through her perspective first. The dual-protagonist structure gives Jason equal gameplay time, but Lucia is the emotional anchor of what Rockstar is building here. Her official bio makes that hierarchy clear without diminishing Jason — they are equals, but she is the one GTA6 is named for in cultural terms.

Cal — The Complication In Jason's Life

Cal
Supporting — Jason's Associate

Cal is officially described in connection with Jason specifically — not with Lucia, not with the broader Vice City criminal world, but with Jason as a personal relationship. In the grammar of GTA storytelling, that specificity matters. Characters who exist in one protagonist's orbit tend to be vehicles for that protagonist's particular conflicts and choices.

Cal reads as the kind of figure who represents the life Jason is either trying to escape, trying to protect, or trying to keep up with. The official description hints at a history between them — this is not a new contact but an established connection that the story will test. Vice City attracts people who are running from something, and Cal's placement in Jason's corner of the narrative suggests he is either part of what Jason is running from or part of what's chasing him.

In a dual-protagonist story, the supporting characters anchored to each lead tend to be the ones who force the hardest decisions. If Lucia has her orbit of characters pulling her in specific directions, Cal appears to be Jason's equivalent pressure point — the person whose presence forces a choice that can't be avoided.

Boobie Ike — Vice City's Power Broker

Boobie Ike
Vice City Criminal Hierarchy

Boobie Ike is one of the most visually prominent figures in both the cover art and the trailers, and the official site description backs up the visual weight with narrative significance. He operates at a level of Vice City's criminal ecosystem that makes him impossible to avoid — not a street-level threat but a structural one. The kind of figure whose permission matters, whose territory exists in formal and informal senses simultaneously.

The cover art placed Boobie Ike in a panel that suggested wealth and confidence without aggression — he is shown in a context of celebration, of presence, of someone who owns a room. The official bio confirms this reading. He is not the snarling antagonist of GTA tradition. He is something more interesting: a man who has built something in Vice City and intends to keep it.

His connection to Jason and Lucia is not yet spelled out explicitly in the official description, but the geography of the story makes it near-certain. Anyone operating at the scale Boobie Ike operates at in Vice City will intersect with two protagonists running jobs in that same city. The question is whether that intersection is collaborative, adversarial, or — most likely in GTA's moral universe — both at different points in the story.

Dre'Quan Priest — A Name That Carries Weight

Dre'Quan Priest
Supporting Character

The surname Priest does a lot of work in the GTA naming tradition, where character names tend to function as compressed biography. Dre'Quan Priest's official description on the Rockstar site positions him as someone operating in Vice City's social landscape with a particular kind of credibility — the kind that comes from authenticity rather than just violence or money.

GTA6's Vice City appears to be built around a more complex social ecosystem than previous entries. Where GTA5's Los Santos was stratified by wealth and industry parody, the Vice City of GTA6 seems to layer in culture, community, and identity in ways that characters like Dre'Quan embody. He is not presented as peripheral. The official description gives him a defined presence and a defined place in the world's social structure.

His connection to the protagonists and to other named characters will become clearer as Rockstar releases more story detail. But the fact that he makes the official named roster — a selective list — signals he plays a genuine role in the narrative rather than functioning as background texture.

Real Dimez: Bae-Luxe and Roxy — Vice City's Most Talked-About Duo

Real Dimez — Bae-Luxe & Roxy
Dual Character / Creator Economy Figures

Real Dimez is the official name for a duo — Bae-Luxe and Roxy — and their placement on the official character roster is one of the most revealing things about what kind of world GTA6 is building. These are characters whose identity is explicitly tied to modern content creation, clout, and the influencer economy. The real Vice City of 2026 is one where social media reach is a form of power, and Rockstar is embedding that reality into the character roster rather than just the background.

Bae-Luxe and Roxy as Real Dimez represent GTA6's most direct engagement with contemporary culture. Where previous GTA games satirised celebrity and media at a remove — through fictional TV shows, radio stations, and background characters — GTA6 is giving the content creator world named, story-connected characters with official biographies. These are not NPCs you walk past. They are people whose arc connects to Jason and Lucia's story.

The naming convention — Real Dimez as the brand, Bae-Luxe and Roxy as the individuals — mirrors how real creator duos and collectives operate. Rockstar has been meticulous about the cultural specificity of Vice City's Leonida setting, and Real Dimez feels like the sharpest expression of that specificity yet. Expect their storyline to touch on fame, authenticity, exploitation, and the particular chaos of being a public figure in a criminal city.

Raul Bautista — The Sinfrontera Connection

Raul Bautista
Sinfrontera National Bank

Raul Bautista is the character most directly telegraphed by the cover art. The panel showing the Sinfrontera National Bank — a striking building name that translates literally to "without border" — placed him in a context of institutional criminality rather than street-level operations. His official bio on the Rockstar site confirms that connection explicitly.

Bautista occupies the institutional layer of Vice City's criminal economy — the part that wears a suit, operates through legitimate-looking structures, and has access to resources that purely street-level players can't touch. In GTA's storytelling tradition, this tier of character tends to be either the primary source of work for the protagonists, the primary source of betrayal, or both in sequence.

The Sinfrontera National Bank as a named institution in the cover art and the official character roster is significant. Banks in GTA have historically been heist locations. A named bank with a named senior figure already established in the official pre-launch materials suggests Sinfrontera is not set dressing — it's a destination. Whether Jason and Lucia end up working for Bautista, robbing from him, or both is the question the story will eventually answer.

Brian Heder — The Wild Card

Brian Heder
Supporting Character

Brian Heder is, at this early stage, the most opaque figure on the official roster. The name itself is deliberately ordinary — a contrast to the Vice City colour of names like Boobie Ike or the cultural specificity of Dre'Quan Priest. In GTA's naming vocabulary, the ordinary name often signals something specific: this is the character who looks like they don't belong in this world and therefore either doesn't survive it or turns out to be exactly what they appear not to be.

The official description positions Heder in relation to the broader Vice City story without placing him in a single defined faction. He is not tied to Bautista's institutional world, not placed in Boobie Ike's criminal hierarchy, not anchored to either protagonist's personal orbit in the explicit way Cal is to Jason. This ambiguity is likely intentional — a character whose allegiances and function in the story remain to be revealed.

In a narrative built around a city full of people running angles, a character whose angle isn't immediately obvious is worth watching carefully. Heder making the official named roster alongside figures as significant as Boobie Ike and Raul Bautista means Rockstar considers him load-bearing to the story. The details will follow — but his placement here is a flag to pay attention.

The Editions — What Rockstar Also Confirmed Today

The character reveal wasn't the only thing that landed on pre-order day. Rockstar also officially confirmed the GTA6 editions alongside the pre-order launch, and two in particular stand out.

✅ Confirmed — Pre-Order Editions

The Ultimate Edition and the Vintage Vice City Pack are both officially confirmed by Rockstar Games as part of the GTA6 pre-order launch. These are not leaks or retailer placeholders — they are on the official product pages live today.

The Ultimate Edition is exactly what it sounds like: the top-tier bundle that packages the base game with additional in-game content, likely including GTA Online currency, bonus items, and early access to story content or cosmetics. Rockstar has run this model successfully since GTA5 and it has only become more refined with Red Dead Redemption 2. The Ultimate Edition is for players who want everything in the box on day one.

The Vintage Vice City Pack is the more interesting of the two confirmed items. The name alone does significant narrative and aesthetic work. Vice City's identity is inseparable from the 1980s — the neon, the pastel suits, the Miami Vice aesthetic that defined the original game's cultural DNA. A Vintage Vice City Pack in a game set in the modern era suggests Rockstar is leaning into that legacy explicitly, offering content that connects modern Leonida to the Vice City players remember. Whether that means retro-styled vehicles, cosmetics, or something more structurally significant in the game world, the naming is deliberate.

Full edition pricing and complete tier breakdowns are now live on the official storefronts. Our pre-order guide is being updated in real time with confirmed pricing as it comes in.

What The Character Roster Tells Us About GTA6's Story

Step back and look at the eight confirmed characters as a collective, and a picture of GTA6's thematic architecture starts to emerge. This is not the traditional GTA protagonist-and-mob-boss structure. The character ecosystem here is more varied, more socially textured, and more deliberately representative of contemporary life in a specific American region than any previous entry in the franchise.

You have two protagonists whose relationship is the story's emotional core. You have a figure in Boobie Ike who represents established criminal power. You have Raul Bautista representing institutional and financial criminality. You have Brian Heder as the ambiguous wild card. You have Dre'Quan Priest as a community-rooted presence. You have Real Dimez as the contemporary creator-economy layer. And you have Cal as the personal complication that makes Jason's choices harder.

Every significant layer of modern Vice City life is represented in this roster. The old money criminal. The new money creator. The street-level loyalist. The institutional figure. The community anchor. The wild card. And at the centre, two people trying to navigate all of it together. That's not a GTA story built around one person's rise and betrayal. That's a story built around a world, and two people trying to survive it without losing each other.

Rockstar has been building to this for over a decade. Today, for the first time, we can see the shape of what they've built. Pre-orders are live. November 19 is locked. And now we know the people we'll be spending it with.
