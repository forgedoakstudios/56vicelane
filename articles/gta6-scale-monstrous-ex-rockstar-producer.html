Ex-Rockstar Producer On GTA6's Scale: "It Must Be Monstrous"

📅 July 18, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 3 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

John Ricchio spent years as a producer at Rockstar Games, working on GTA5, Red Dead Redemption, and Max Payne 3 before leaving the studio. Asked recently whether he'd want to be part of the team building GTA6, his answer doubled as a pretty good gut-check on just how big this game is shaping up to be.

⚠️ The Quote

"I think the fact that every single time they do a GTA, it's like ludicrously bigger than before is something that I think about. Like, I can only imagine the size of GTA6. Like it must be monstrous. Or if not monstrous, it's probably way more detailed." — John Ricchio

Ricchio made the comment during a wide-ranging appearance on the Kiwi Talkz podcast (episode #212), covering his time at Rockstar — leadership, QA, crunch culture, and what it was actually like working under Sam and Dan Houser. The GTA6 comment came up when he was asked, point blank, whether he'd want to be on the team right now.

"Pretty Happy" To Be Sitting This One Out

His answer wasn't just about scale — it was about what that scale costs the people building it. Ricchio pointed out that GTA5 was already "significantly bigger than GTA4," and every entry since has followed the same pattern: each game dwarfs the one before it. Extrapolate that forward to GTA6, and you get exactly the reaction he had.

He laughed it off, saying he's "pretty happy" he's not on GTA6, and was candid about why: "it is really difficult, and it's a hard period of your career when you're working that hard on something." Coming from someone who was actually inside Rockstar for multiple GTA and Red Dead cycles, that's less an outsider's guess and more a description of what shipping a game at this scale is actually like from the inside.

Not the First Time He's Talked Rockstar's Process

Ricchio has been fairly open in this same interview about Rockstar's development philosophy more broadly, including why GTA6 is launching console-only rather than day-one on PC — arguing it's a resource-management call, not an anti-PC one: "you're much better off starting with the constraints," since "shrinking is a lot harder than extending." It's a window into how deliberately Rockstar scopes these projects, which makes his read on GTA6's raw scale carry a bit more weight than typical outsider speculation.

ℹ️ Worth Remembering

Ricchio left Rockstar before GTA6's development cycle, so this is an informed outsider's read — not insider knowledge of the actual game. Nothing here is a leak or a confirmed detail about GTA6 itself, just a veteran producer's honest reaction to the trajectory the series has been on for two decades.

Every mainline GTA since San Andreas has been a bigger undertaking than the last, and Rockstar has never really given fans a reason to expect that trend to break. Ricchio's comment is really just a former insider saying the quiet part out loud: even the people who used to make these games can't fully picture what "bigger than GTA5" looks like in practice.
