GTA 6 Trailer 3: Everything We Need To See Before Pre-Orders Drop June 25

📅 June 22, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 10 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

Three days from now, GTA 6 pre-orders go live. June 25 is the date Rockstar Games locked in publicly when they dropped the cover art announcement — and the entire GTA community is expecting one more thing to land alongside it: Trailer 3.

The logic is simple. Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick said the marketing campaign wouldn't kick off until summer. Summer officially started June 21. Pre-orders opening is the single biggest marketing moment before launch day itself. Rockstar is not going to open the wallets of millions of fans without giving them something to be excited about first. Trailer 3 and June 25 are locked together in everyone's mind — and for good reason.

So here's the real question: what does Trailer 3 actually need to show us? Two trailers in, we have a stunning picture of the world, a solid grip on who Jason and Lucia are, and almost nothing about how the game actually plays. That's the gap Trailer 3 has to close. Here's everything on the list.

ℹ️ What's Confirmed vs Speculation

Rockstar has not officially confirmed that Trailer 3 drops on June 25. The connection between the pre-order date and a trailer is widely expected based on Zelnick's comments and Rockstar's historical marketing patterns — but it is not locked. Everything in this article about Trailer 3's content is community expectation and analysis, not confirmed fact.

What Trailers 1 and 2 Actually Showed Us

Before getting into what we need from Trailer 3, it's worth being honest about what the first two actually delivered — because the gap between what people think they know about GTA 6 and what Rockstar has officially confirmed is wider than most fans realize.

Trailer 1 (December 2023) was 91 seconds of pure atmosphere. Tom Petty's "Love Is a Long Road" played over a montage of Leonida life — wildlife, suburbs, swamps, beachfronts, a traffic stop, a nightclub. Lucia appeared in a prison jumpsuit. We got the setting, the tone, and the first look at a female protagonist. We got almost no story and zero Jason. It was a place-setting piece — and a masterpiece at its job.

Trailer 2 (May 2025) ran nearly three minutes and went significantly deeper. Jason Duval got his name and his first real screen time. We saw the two together — Jason picking Lucia up from Leonida Penitentiary, the two of them robbing Sinfrontera National Bank with skull masks, driving together, a yacht, a beach pier, a local bar, the keys. The trailer confirmed the Bonnie and Clyde dynamic, introduced supporting characters including drug smuggler Brian Heder and local legend Boobie Ike, and showed the breadth of Leonida as a setting.

What neither trailer showed: a single second of raw, unscripted gameplay. Everything we've seen is cinematic or clearly choreographed engine footage. The world looks extraordinary. We still don't know how it actually feels to play.

The Big Question: Will We Finally See Gameplay?

This is the one everyone is asking. Two and a half years since the first reveal, we have not seen one unscripted moment of GTA 6 in action. Every major GTA channel on YouTube right now is asking the same thing: is Trailer 3 finally the gameplay trailer?

There are strong reasons to think yes. Rockstar confirmed to press that review copies will not be distributed ahead of launch — journalists will only see the game at supervised preview events. That means the community's only real window into gameplay before launch day is whatever Rockstar chooses to show in trailers and preview materials. With pre-orders opening, Rockstar needs people to feel confident spending money on a game they've never touched. Showing gameplay is the obvious way to do that.

What gameplay would mean in practical terms: driving mechanics, gunplay, the character switch in action, what open-world exploration actually looks like, how the wanted system responds, what an NPC interaction feels like at street level. Trailer 2 teased vehicles, motorcycles, helicopters, speedboats — but it was all cinematic. We want to see the wheel in someone's hands.

✅ What's Already Confirmed About Gameplay

Dual protagonist switching — confirmed. Jason has a slow-motion weak point ability. Lucia has a single-shot slow-motion mode. Prone crawling was cut from the final build. Dual wielding was also cut. The weapon wheel returns. Over 200 vehicles confirmed. Zip ties, human shields, and a reworked wanted system are confirmed. Character hair and facial hair grow over time like RDR2.

How Does the Dual Protagonist Switch Actually Work?

GTA 5 had three protagonists and a character wheel that let you snap between Michael, Franklin, and Trevor from almost anywhere in the world. The switch was a zoom-out animation, a camera drop from orbit, and you landed in whatever ridiculous situation your other character had drifted into.

GTA 6 has two protagonists who are almost always together. That changes the mechanic completely. According to what's been revealed and credibly reported, the character selection wheel has three modes: Solo Jason, Solo Lucia, and a dual control mode that lets both characters operate simultaneously during open-world activities and certain missions. The switch between Jason and Lucia when they're together is reportedly instant — no zoom-out animation, no orbital drop. The SSD speeds of PS5 and Xbox Series X make that possible in a way it wasn't on last generation.

We haven't seen this demonstrated. We need to. If Rockstar shows Trailer 3 and still doesn't show how the switch actually works mid-mission or in the open world, that's going to be the loudest conversation in the community the next day.

The Story Questions That Still Haven't Been Answered

Both trailers leaned hard on atmosphere and character chemistry. We know the broad strokes: Jason and Lucia are a Bonnie and Clyde-style criminal duo caught in a conspiracy stretching across the state of Leonida after an easy score goes wrong. We know Jason spent time in the Army before ending up in the Keys working for Brian Heder's drug operation. We know Lucia served time at Leonida Penitentiary. We know they're both fighting for a way out.

What we don't know: What was the heist that went wrong? Who is the actual antagonist — Heder, someone higher up, a law enforcement figure, something else entirely? What does "a conspiracy stretching across the state" actually mean in terms of factions, organizations, and power structures? How deep does the story go before it becomes the typical GTA power climb?

Rockstar's official story description frames them as "two outsiders trying to make their way through the seedy streets of modern Vice City and Leonida." That's a Bonnie and Clyde opening. What Trailer 3 needs to deliver is the first real glimpse of the stakes — what they're running from, who's hunting them, and what they're willing to do to get out.

Vice City: What We've Seen and What's Still Hidden

Trailer 2 showed us wide-angle shots of Vice City's skyline, neon signs, nightclubs, strip clubs, a Ferris wheel, high-rise apartments, beach boardwalks, and Art Deco architecture. It looked stunning. It also looked like a highlight reel — carefully selected shots from the most photogenic parts of the map.

Leonida is confirmed to be significantly larger than GTA 5's map — credible estimates put it at roughly 1.5 to 2 times the size, with over 700 enterable interiors confirmed. The state includes Vice City proper, the Leonida Keys, the Grassrivers (an Everglades-style biome that's generating serious buzz on YouTube right now), Port Gellhorn, Ambrosia, and Mount Kalaga National Park. We've seen Vice City beach. We've seen flashes of the swamp. We've seen the Keys.

What Trailer 3 should show: the full scope. Not just the neon-lit tourist brochure version of Vice City, but the sprawl — the interstate highways cutting through flat Florida scrubland, the rural poverty of the interior, the chaos of the Grassrivers, the contrast between the Keys' luxury marina scene and the industrial port towns. The world of Leonida is the real protagonist of GTA 6 in a lot of ways. Show us all of it.

The Price Question Nobody Wants to Ask

Here's the uncomfortable one. Pre-orders open June 25. That means June 25 is also the day we find out what GTA 6 actually costs. And based on everything that's been signaled, it isn't going to be $70.

Strauss Zelnick has previously referenced a $70 to $80 range for major releases. Bank of America analysts have publicly suggested that a game of GTA 6's scale could support an $80 standard price — and potentially reset AAA pricing expectations for the entire industry. GTA Online is still one of the highest-grossing live service games on the planet in 2026. Take-Two has financial leverage here that no other publisher has.

The community reaction to an $80 price point is going to be complicated. A lot of players will pay it without blinking. A significant portion will use it as a flashpoint. Trailer 3 dropping the same day as the price reveal is not an accident — Rockstar wants the emotional response to the trailer to do the work of justifying the number. If the trailer is extraordinary, the price conversation shifts. If the trailer is underwhelming, the price is all anyone talks about.

⚠️ Heads Up

No official price has been confirmed as of June 22, 2026. The $70–$80 range is based on Zelnick's public statements and analyst projections. The actual standard edition price will be revealed when pre-orders open June 25.

GTA Online 2: The Question Rockstar Is Deliberately Avoiding

GTA Online has been printing money for Take-Two for over a decade. GTA+ memberships were still growing year-over-year heading into the launch window. That ecosystem is enormous and the people invested in it have a very specific question: what happens to my character, my properties, my vehicles, my money?

Everything credibly reported points to a clean break. GTA 6 Online is expected to be a separate world — a new Leonida with a fresh economy, no character transfers, no asset transfers, no money carries. The rationale is sound: GTA Online's economy has been inflated beyond recognition. Modded money lobbies, Shark Cards, years of compound accumulation. Starting fresh is the only way to build a functional new economy from day one.

But Rockstar hasn't said any of this officially. They haven't confirmed the name GTA Online 2. They haven't confirmed a launch window for the online component. They haven't said a single word about whether the online component ships November 19 or follows single-player like GTA Online followed GTA 5 back in 2013.

If Trailer 3 shows even a single second of multiplayer footage — a lobby, a heist coordination screen, anything — it will be the biggest moment in gaming in years. The GTA Online community is enormous and they need answers. June 25 is the moment to give them.

The Supporting Cast: Brian Heder, Boobie Ike, and Who Else?

Trailer 2 introduced us to the supporting cast in broad strokes. Brian Heder is the veteran drug smuggler operating out of the Keys who gives Jason a place to stay in exchange for running his errands — the man who essentially pulled Jason into the criminal world Lucia then detonates. Boobie Ike is a Vice City local legend who turned street hustle into a business empire that includes real estate, a strip club, and a recording label called Only Raw Records. Cal Hampton is Jason's Keys friend who joins them at the local bar.

These characters got name-drops and glimpses. They didn't get depth. One of the things the community is most hungry for ahead of Trailer 3 is a clearer picture of the antagonist structure. Every great GTA story has a clear villain hierarchy. GTA 5 had Devin Weston and Steve Haines. GTA: San Andreas had Officer Tenpenny. GTA: Vice City had Sonny Forelli. Who is GTA 6's villain? Is it Heder? Is it someone above Heder? Is it a law enforcement corruption story? A cartel? A conspiracy that goes all the way to the top of Leonida's political structure?

The story tagline — "the darkest side of the sunniest place in America, in the middle of a conspiracy stretching across the state" — is promising something bigger than a drug running operation. Trailer 3 needs to start pulling that thread.

What Would Make Trailer 3 Perfect

Here's the list. Not a wishlist — the actual things that would make this the definitive marketing moment and justify the pre-order numbers Rockstar is aiming for:

Raw gameplay footage. Not cinematic. Not scripted showcase. Someone driving in traffic, getting into a gunfight, switching between Jason and Lucia mid-chase, surviving a wanted level. Real gameplay. Even 60 seconds of it would change the conversation entirely.

The character switch in action. Show the wheel. Show the instant swap. Show what dual control looks like. This is the mechanic that separates GTA 6 from everything that came before it and nobody has seen it work yet.

A glimpse of the actual story stakes. Not just atmosphere. Give us the inciting incident. Give us the moment everything goes wrong. Give us the face of whoever Jason and Lucia are running from.

A map tease. Even a partial map reveal would break the internet. The community has been building fan maps from trailer coordinates and leak data for three years. Show us something official and the reaction will be enormous.

GTA Online confirmation. Even one sentence. Even just confirmation that it's coming. The GTA Online community is waiting.

Five things. Any one of them makes Trailer 3 a massive moment. All five would make it the most-watched gaming video in YouTube history.

Why June 25 Is Bigger Than Just Pre-Orders

There's a reason every major game publisher has already cleared out of November 2026. Microsoft delayed Fable. Tomb Raider: Legacy of Atlantis moved to avoid the window. The industry has organized its release calendar around a single date. November 19 isn't just a GTA launch. It's the pivot point the entire gaming industry is rotating around this year.

June 25 is the moment the final arc begins. The moment pre-orders open, the launch becomes real in a way it wasn't before. The money is committed. The hype machine moves from theoretical to active. Marketing ramps up through summer and doesn't stop until the midnight launch.

And if Trailer 3 drops that same day — if Rockstar walks out and says here's the gameplay, here's the price, here's when online launches, here's pre-order now — it becomes the moment the gaming world officially holds its breath until November 19.

We've been waiting since December 2023. The wait is almost over. And the next 72 hours might just be the most important marketing moment in gaming history.
