GTA6 YouTuber Allegedly Gets Police Called After Walking Into Rockstar Games Lobby

📅 July 22, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 5 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

One GTA6 YouTuber learned a hard lesson about the difference between online content and real-world consequences. The creator allegedly showed up at Rockstar Games' lobby, prompting staff to call law enforcement over concerns about the uninvited visit.

🎥 What actually happened

The creator behind the channel ÜberGaming filmed himself and a small group walking directly into the lobby of Rockstar North's Edinburgh headquarters — Rockstar's original studio and one of the key teams behind GTA6 — and posted the footage on June 2. According to the video, the visit itself happened on May 26, a date that means something specific to anyone following this game closely: it was the originally planned GTA6 release date, before Rockstar pushed the launch back a second time to November 19.

The group reportedly stayed at a hotel across the street from Rockstar North's office beforehand, then walked in and started filming. They didn't get far. Security staff confronted them within seconds of the camera rolling and asked them to leave.

👮 Then the police showed up

That's where this stopped being a harmless prank video. Officers arrived at the scene, took down identification for everyone in the group, and questioned them about their intentions. Per the creator's own account, police delivered one very pointed message on the way out: if anything from GTA6 ever leaks, Rockstar and the authorities will know exactly who to talk to first.

The YouTuber, for his part, tried to play it for laughs afterward — joking that he'd earned "five stars in real life with Rockstar Games," a nod to the in-game wanted-level system. Funny line. Considerably less funny if you're the security team at a studio currently sitting on the most anticipated game in the industry's history, fielding an uninvited walk-in during the exact week the internet is primed to assume anything unusual means a leak.

📌 Why This Isn't Actually Funny

Rockstar North isn't a theme park attraction — it's a workplace, currently operating under enormous pressure and security scrutiny ahead of a launch that's been years in the making. A stunt like this isn't just a risk to the person filming it; it puts real strain on the actual people who show up to build this game every day, during what's already one of the most stressful stretches of their careers.

🌍 Part of a bigger pattern

This isn't the first time GTA6 hype has spilled over into real-world stunts near Rockstar's offices, and it likely won't be the last before November 19. The closer launch gets, the more content creators are going to look for exactly this kind of attention-grabbing moment — proximity to Rockstar, however brief or staged, reads as instant engagement online. That incentive isn't going away, which means studios like Rockstar North are only going to get more cautious, not less, between now and launch.

The bottom line

While hype for GTA6 is at fever pitch, this incident serves as a stark reminder: the developers behind Vice City deserve to work without harassment. Content creation ends where people's safety and workplaces begin. Keep it online, keep it respectful.
