Rockstar Won’t Send GTA6 Review Copies — What That Means For Day-One Buyers

Rockstar Games reportedly will not distribute GTA6 review copies to press ahead of launch. Journalists will preview under strict supervision at a closed event instead. If this holds, there may be no review scores available when you decide whether to spend $100+ on day one.

May 18, 2026  •  56ViceLane Staff  •  Press · Reviews · Launch

What Review Copies Are And Why They Matter

Review copies are standard operating procedure for virtually every major game release. Publishers send early builds to press outlets so reviews go live at or near launch — giving consumers information before they spend their money. It has been the industry norm for decades, and review scores on Metacritic and OpenCritic directly influence day-one sales numbers.

Rockstar choosing to bypass this process entirely — if the rumor holds — would be a significant departure from that norm. And given everything Rockstar has been through with GTA6 security, the reasoning is not hard to understand.

Why Rockstar Would Make This Call

GTA6 has been one of the most leaked games in development history. Footage from early builds was famously leaked during development, forcing Rockstar to publicly confirm the game years before they were ready to. The source code for GTA5 was also leaked in a separate incident. Every time sensitive material escaped, it caused significant damage and disruption.

Sending review copies to dozens of press outlets weeks before launch creates dozens of potential leak points. A single reviewer posting early footage, screenshots, or story spoilers could unravel years of careful marketing management. The controlled preview event model eliminates that risk entirely.

"Rockstar will not distribute GTA6 review copies to prevent leaks. Journalists will preview the game under strict supervision."

— ResetEra report, May 2026

How Supervised Press Events Work

The alternative — sometimes called a review camp — involves inviting press to a specific location to play the game on-site under supervision. Embargo lifts simultaneously for all outlets, typically at or just after launch. Footage recorded at the event stays controlled. Reviewers write what they experienced but cannot post raw gameplay.

This approach is not unprecedented. Some publishers have used it for games with major spoiler risk. But for a game the size of GTA6, doing this at the scale required — accommodating enough press to generate meaningful launch coverage globally — is a significant logistical undertaking.

What This Means If You Are Buying Day One

The practical impact depends on whether Rockstar holds the embargo past launch. There are two scenarios:

Embargo lifts at launch: Reviews go live the same day the game does. You still get coverage, just not the weeks-in-advance window that helps publishers build pre-launch momentum. For a game like GTA6, pre-launch momentum is not something Rockstar needs.

Embargo lifts after launch: You are buying blind. No Metacritic score, no review consensus, no professional opinion to guide your decision. Given the price point this game is likely to carry, that puts more pressure on day-one buyers.

The reality

GTA6 will sell tens of millions of copies regardless of what reviews say. Rockstar knows this. The review copy decision is about protecting the launch experience — not about hiding a bad game. No GTA mainline entry has ever received a bad critical reception.

The Leak Context

The 2022 GTA6 development footage leak was one of the largest in gaming history. Over 90 clips from an early build appeared online, showing unfinished gameplay and character animations. The hacker was later arrested, but the damage to Rockstar's controlled reveal strategy was done.

That context makes this decision understandable even if it frustrates journalists and reviewers. Rockstar is protecting the most valuable unreleased media property in the world. Sending that to dozens of external parties weeks before launch is a risk the company has clearly decided is not worth taking.

Still A Rumor

This remains unconfirmed by Rockstar or Take-Two. It fits the pattern of a studio that is more protective of its work than almost any other in the industry — but until official press communications go out, treat this as a credible rumor, not confirmed policy.
