GTA V Story Mode Added To GTA+ — What It Really Means With 159 Days Until GTA6

56ViceLane News Desk 📅 June 12, 2026 ⏱️ 6 min read
Share Reddit Post on X

Rockstar just made GTA V Story Mode available through GTA+ — their $7.99/month subscription service. For players who never owned the story campaign, they can now access it without buying the game outright. With 159 days left until GTA6 launches November 19, the timing of this move is not accidental.

The news broke June 11, 2026, confirmed by RockstarINTEL. The move follows GTA Online being added to the GTA+ Games Library several months prior. Now both pillars of GTA V — the story and the online experience — are inside the subscription ecosystem. For $7.99 a month, you get the full Michael, Trevor, and Franklin saga plus everything that comes with GTA+ membership.

Why Rockstar Did This Right Now

The timing is deliberate. With GTA6 launching November 19, Rockstar needs the GTA ecosystem as active as possible heading into launch. A larger active playerbase means more people already engaged with Rockstar's world when GTA6 drops. More pre-orders. More hype. More social conversation. Getting GTA V's story into GTA+ brings in players who own consoles but never picked up the game — or newer PS5 and Xbox Series owners who skipped the PS4/Xbox One era entirely.

There is also a narrative reason. GTA V's story introduces Michael, Trevor, and Franklin — the characters who defined a generation of open world gaming. Getting new players through that story before GTA6 arrives with Jason and Lucia builds emotional context. Rockstar is not just selling subscriptions. They are onboarding the next wave of the community.

ℹ️ Platform Note

GTA+ is only available on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC Enhanced. PS4 and Xbox One players cannot access the subscription. If you are on last-gen hardware, the story mode purchase still applies through the standard store.

What This Means For The Last Drive

Here is the angle that matters to this community. The Last Drive is scheduled for November 18, 2026 — the night before GTA6 launches. It is a free community farewell cruise through GTA Online. No killing, no griefing, just thousands of players riding together one final time.

This GTA+ move means more players will be active in GTA Online heading into November. Players who subscribed to experience the story mode will still have active GTA Online access through their membership. That is a larger potential audience for The Last Drive than existed a month ago.

✅ Last Drive Impact

More players in GTA Online heading into November = a bigger Last Drive. If you joined through GTA+, your spot on the Wall of Honor is waiting at 56ViceLane.com/lastdrive. Sign up free — no purchase required.

The GTA+ Library Is Getting Serious

Before this announcement, the GTA+ Games Library already included Red Dead Redemption, GTA Online, LA Noire, the GTA Trilogy Definitive Edition, Bully, GTA Chinatown Wars, GTA Liberty City Stories, and GTA Vice City. Adding GTA V Story Mode is the crown jewel of that lineup. This is now a legitimate gaming subscription with one of the most played open world games ever included at the base tier.

The comparison to Game Pass and PS Plus becomes sharper. Those services rotate games. GTA+ keeps them permanently in the library. For players who want consistent access to the Rockstar catalog, the $7.99 price point starts looking rational — especially with GTA6 incoming and presumably a next wave of GTA+ content tied to Leonida.

Progress Migration — Your Save Data Is Safe

One detail worth highlighting for longtime players: Rockstar has confirmed that Story Mode and Online progress migrates cleanly to the latest versions on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC Enhanced. If you played on last gen and moved to current hardware, your hours are not lost. The migration preserves career progress, properties, vehicles, and the Hao's Special Works upgrades system.

This matters for The Last Drive. Players who migrated their characters to current gen will be in fully equipped sessions on November 18. Their cars. Their garages. Their tuned builds. All of it carries over for the final cruise.

Is This Rockstar Saying Goodbye To GTA V?

That is the question worth sitting with. When a game moves into a subscription library at the base tier, it typically signals that the primary commercial life of that title is winding down. Putting GTA V Story Mode into GTA+ is Rockstar acknowledging that the era of selling that game at full price is effectively over. They are transitioning from selling GTA V to using it as a subscriber acquisition tool for the GTA+ ecosystem.

It is not a sad moment. It is a passing of the torch. GTA V has sold over 200 million copies across three console generations. It does not need to be sold anymore. Now it serves a new purpose — keeping the Rockstar world alive while GTA6 gets ready to take over. The city that raised us is becoming the welcome mat for what comes next.

⚠️ Important Restriction

Playing GTA Online through GTA+ still requires an active PlayStation Plus or Xbox Game Pass subscription on top of the GTA+ fee. The $7.99 alone covers Story Mode access — Online requires the extra layer. Factor that into your subscription calculation.

The 56ViceLane Take

Rockstar adding GTA V to GTA+ is the clearest signal yet that the transition to GTA6 is fully underway. The commercial chapter of GTA V is closing. The community chapter — The Last Drive, the Wall of Honor, the final cruise on November 18 — is the one that matters now.

If this news brought new players into GTA Online, or brought back lapsed players through a subscription, then the potential audience for The Last Drive just grew. That is good. The bigger the convoy, the more appropriate the send-off for a game that defined a generation.

🏁 The Bottom Line

GTA V Story Mode in GTA+ at $7.99/month is Rockstar's final salute to the game that carried them for 13 years. It brings new players in, keeps the ecosystem active, and sets the stage for the GTA6 era. For The Last Drive community — more players in GTA Online heading into November is nothing but good news.
