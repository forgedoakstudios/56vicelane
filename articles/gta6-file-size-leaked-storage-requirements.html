GTA6 File Size Leaked — And Your PS5 Might Not Be Ready

📅 June 8, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 8 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

Just when you thought you had your PlayStation 5 setup dialed in — a new leak drops that might send you scrambling to the electronics store before November 19th.

According to reports circulating across multiple gaming sources, Grand Theft Auto 6 could arrive with a file size of just under 200 gigabytes. Let that sink in for a moment. 200 gigabytes. On a console that ships with 825GB of total storage — of which only around 667GB is actually usable — that means GTA6 alone could consume nearly 30 percent of your entire drive before you even download a single patch.

And here is the kicker — that number could go up before launch day.

This is the GTA6 storage conversation the community needs to start having right now. Not in October. Not in November. Now. Because if you wait until launch day to figure out your storage situation you are going to be sitting on your couch watching a progress bar for hours while everyone else is already in Los Santos.

⚠️ Head Up

Everything in this article regarding file size is based on unconfirmed leaks. Rockstar has not officially announced GTA6 storage requirements. Plan around these numbers but treat them as estimates until official confirmation.

Where Did This Number Come From?

The 200GB figure originated from a post on X — formerly Twitter — by an account called GTA 6 Intel, which has a track record of sharing information that later proved accurate. Since that original post, multiple additional sources have pointed to similar numbers, with some suggesting the figure has remained stable through Rockstar's development process.

The fact that two independent sources landed on the same number gives this leak more credibility than most. But Rockstar has confirmed absolutely nothing about file size. Everything here is unconfirmed leak territory — take it seriously enough to prepare but do not treat it as confirmed fact until Rockstar speaks officially.

What we do know is that games have been getting larger with every generation. Red Dead Redemption 2 launched at around 99GB on PS4. GTA5 Enhanced Edition on PS5 sits at roughly 75GB. A next generation open world game with Vice City's reported scale, dynamic weather systems, hundreds of enterable buildings, and a dual protagonist story could absolutely justify pushing toward 200GB and beyond.

The math is not shocking. The reality of what it means for your storage situation is.

The PS5 Storage Problem Nobody Talks About

Here is a number that should concern every PS5 owner right now. Your PlayStation 5 comes with 825GB of built-in SSD storage. Sounds like plenty until you realize that Sony uses a significant portion of that for system software and overhead, leaving you with approximately 667GB of usable space.

Now look at what a typical PS5 library looks like for an active gamer. Call of Duty titles routinely push 150-200GB on their own. EA Sports titles take up 50GB plus. Spider-Man 2 is around 75GB. Hogwarts Legacy is 75GB. Elden Ring, God of War, Horizon — all in the 40-75GB range. By the time you have four or five major titles installed you are already looking at 400-500GB used.

Add GTA6 at 200GB and you have a math problem. Something has to go. And for most players that something is going to require a serious look at their storage situation before November 19th arrives.

Will The File Size Get Bigger Before Launch?

Possibly. Probably. Here is how game development works in the final months before a major release. The core game is essentially complete but the development team is still adding polish, fixing bugs, optimizing performance, and building day one patches. All of that adds data.

When Red Dead Redemption 2 launched the day one patch alone was substantial. Rockstar is reportedly planning an aggressive marketing campaign through the summer — which means system requirements, storage details, and pricing information will likely drop in the coming months. When that happens the 200GB figure either gets confirmed, adjusted upward, or surprises everyone by coming in lower.

Until then smart players are treating 200GB as the floor, not the ceiling, when planning their storage strategy.

Your Storage Options Right Now

So what do you actually do about this? Here are your real options laid out clearly.

Option 1 — Delete and Manage

The free option. Go through your PS5 library right now and be ruthless. Games you have not touched in six months get deleted. You can always redownload them later. This is fine as a short term strategy but if you have a deep library it means making hard choices and you will face the same problem again when GTA6 Online updates start rolling in.

Option 2 — Expand With an M.2 SSD

The PS5 supports M.2 SSD expansion in the second slot. Installing a compatible drive gives you anywhere from 1TB to 4TB of additional high-speed storage. This is the clean solution for serious players. Prices on M.2 SSDs have dropped significantly and a quality 2TB drive that meets PlayStation's speed requirements can be found at solid prices right now.

The key requirement is that the drive needs to meet PS5's minimum read speed of 5,500MB/s. Not every M.2 drive on the market qualifies so make sure you check compatibility before buying. The Samsung 990 Pro and WD Black SN850X are two of the most commonly recommended options that meet the spec.

Option 3 — External USB Storage

PS5 does not support playing PS5 games from external USB storage — only PS4 games. So this option works for clearing out your PS4 back catalog to free up internal space but will not help you store GTA6 itself.

Option 4 — Wait and See

Valid if you want to wait for official confirmation before spending money. Just understand that if you wait until October or November the prices on compatible SSDs may spike as demand rises around launch windows. Supply chain issues around major game launches are real and have happened before.

The Xbox Situation

Xbox Series X owners are in a similar but slightly different position. The Series X ships with 1TB of NVMe storage with around 802GB usable — better than PS5's baseline but still potentially tight with a 200GB game eating into it.

Xbox uses a proprietary storage expansion card system rather than standard M.2, which means your expansion options are more limited and generally more expensive. The official Seagate Storage Expansion Cards for Xbox are the primary certified option, available in 512GB, 1TB, and 2TB sizes.

If you are on Xbox Series S — the 512GB model — a 200GB GTA6 install is going to be painful. You will almost certainly need a storage expansion before launch day if you want to keep any other games installed alongside it.

What About PC Players?

PC players have it easiest here. Storage expansion on PC is straightforward, options are abundant, and prices per gigabyte are lower than console solutions. A 2TB NVMe SSD for PC can be found at very competitive prices and installation is typically simple.

The more relevant question for PC players is whether their rig can actually run GTA6 at a level that does justice to what Rockstar has built. Rockstar has confirmed that a PC version is coming — though timing suggests it may arrive in 2027, roughly a year after the PS5 launch. For PC players preparing their builds for the eventual PC release our GTA6 PC Build Guide covers everything you need to know.

What 200GB Tells Us About GTA6

Here is the thing about a 200GB file size that does not get discussed enough. It is actually a sign of how ambitious this game is.

Rockstar has been in development on GTA6 for over a decade. The reported features — a massive modern Vice City, 700 plus enterable buildings, dynamic weather, dual protagonists, body changes based on lifestyle, deeper NPC behavior, trunk storage systems, a six-star wanted escalation — all of that takes data. Enormous amounts of data.

The compression technology Rockstar uses is reportedly doing its job well to keep the game at this size. When you look at it that way 200GB is not a problem. It is a promise. It is Rockstar telling you that they built something so large, so detailed, and so alive that it fills nearly a quarter of your console's drive.

GTA6 is not going to be an upgrade of GTA5. It is going to be a different category of game entirely. And 200GB might be exactly what that costs.
