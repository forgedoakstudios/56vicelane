GTA6 Pre-Order Claims Are "Absurd," Analyst Firm Says — But It's Still History's Strongest Campaign

📅 July 17, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 5 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

The numbers flying around GTA6 pre-orders this week have gotten wild enough that the analytics firm behind the real data felt the need to step in and correct the record. Market analytics firm Newzoo has directly pushed back on viral claims circulating on social media that GTA6 has already pulled in 39 million pre-orders and $3 billion in pre-launch revenue — calling those specific numbers false. But in the same breath, Newzoo confirmed the real figures still make this the strongest pre-order campaign the games industry has ever recorded.

⚠️ The Quote

"Contrary to social media reports, GTA 6 has not done a billion dollars in pre-orders 21 weeks out. This is absurd. Given how pre-order curves look, nothing ever has and nothing ever will in the near future." — Newzoo

That's about as direct as an analyst firm's public correction gets. Newzoo isn't hedging or softening the language — it's calling the viral billion-dollar framing out by name, and explaining exactly why it doesn't hold up: pre-order revenue simply doesn't behave the way the viral posts imply, for any game, ever, based on how these curves have historically played out industry-wide.

What Was Actually Being Claimed

The viral numbers making the rounds this week put GTA6 at roughly 39 million pre-orders and $3 billion in revenue from pre-orders alone, weeks before the game has even launched. Multiple outlets, including Gaming Amigos and KhelNow, ran their own fact-checks on the 39-million figure this week as it spread — and the consistent conclusion across that reporting is that the number does not trace back to any verifiable source. It reads like a rounding-up of real, smaller figures into something more dramatic.

Newzoo's own framing is blunt about why: no game has ever cleared a billion dollars in pre-order revenue specifically, regardless of how anticipated it was. Pre-orders are historically a fraction of total buyers — most people wait for release, reviews, and word of mouth before purchasing. A billion-dollar pre-order haul would require a level of advance commitment that simply doesn't match how players actually buy games, even for a release as anticipated as this one.

So What Are The Real Numbers?

This is where it gets more interesting, because Newzoo isn't saying GTA6's pre-order performance is unremarkable — quite the opposite. By their estimate, GTA6 pulled in roughly $180 million in combined digital pre-order sales across the US and the top five European markets in the first week pre-orders were live. Once you account for markets outside that core sample, Newzoo puts worldwide week-one pre-order sales as high as $260 million — a real, if far less headline-grabbing, number than the viral claims.

From there, Newzoo's own projection is that GTA6 is on pace for $3.3 to $5.2 billion in cumulative global revenue by the end of launch week itself — once actual sales are added to pre-orders after the game ships November 19. We broke that fuller picture down in yesterday's deep dive on the record pre-order numbers, including how it stacks up against GTA5's own launch-week performance back in 2013.

Why This Distinction Actually Matters

It would be easy to read this as analysts "debunking" GTA6 hype, but that's not what's happening here. Newzoo is doing the opposite of deflating the story — it's correcting an inflated, fabricated number while confirming the real, verified number is still historic. $260 million in pre-orders alone, weeks before launch, with a legitimate path to being the biggest games launch week ever recorded, doesn't need help from a made-up billion-dollar figure to be a genuinely enormous story.

If anything, the viral misinformation risks undercutting the real achievement here. When the real number ($260 million, strongest ever recorded) gets buried under a fake one ($3 billion, physically implausible per historical pre-order behavior), and that fake number later gets debunked, the whole story risks reading as "GTA6 pre-order hype was overblown" — when the accurate version of the story is that GTA6's pre-order campaign is legitimately breaking records without any exaggeration required.

✅ The Bottom Line

If you see the 39 million pre-orders or $3 billion figure being shared, it's not accurate — Newzoo has directly and publicly called it out. The real, verified figure (~$260 million in worldwide week-one pre-orders) is still a genuine record on its own terms, and the $3.3–5.2 billion range is a launch-week revenue projection, not a pre-order number. Both things are true at once: the viral number is fake, and the real story is still historic.

The Pattern Worth Watching For

This is unlikely to be the last inflated claim to circulate before November 19. Major launches with this much anticipation tend to attract exaggerated numbers precisely because the real story is already big enough to seem plausible when someone rounds it up further. The practical takeaway: when a GTA6 sales figure sounds too extreme to be true, it's worth checking whether an actual analytics firm — Newzoo, NPD, GSD, or similar — has attached their name to it, rather than a screenshot circulating without a source.

Whatever the real final numbers end up being when Take-Two actually reports them, the underlying trend doesn't need inflating. GTA6 heads into launch week with the strongest pre-order campaign the industry has ever tracked — verified, real, and headline-worthy entirely on its own.
