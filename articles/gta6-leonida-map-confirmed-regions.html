The GTA6 Leonida Map — Every Confirmed Region Broken Down

May 30, 2026  ·  56ViceLane News Desk

Sources: Rockstar Games official GTA VI page · Official Trailer 1 and Trailer 2 · Official screenshots released May 2025 · All region descriptions based on official Rockstar content only.

Fan-made maps are everywhere right now. Some are impressive. Some are completely fabricated. Most mix confirmed Rockstar information with community speculation and present it all as fact. That's not what this is. This is the breakdown of exactly what Rockstar has officially confirmed about the state of Leonida — six named regions, shown in official trailers and screenshots, listed on the Rockstar Games website. Nothing more. Nothing less. Every claim tagged so you know exactly what you're reading.

⚠️ About Those Fan Maps Circulating On Social Media

The detailed maps showing specific roads, city layouts, and precise region boundaries are fan creations — not official Rockstar releases. Some are impressive community projects built from trailer analysis. None of them are confirmed by Rockstar. We cover what Rockstar has actually shown. Fan speculation is labeled clearly.

The Six Confirmed Regions ✅

Rockstar's official GTA VI website and trailer content confirms six named regions across the state of Leonida. Here's each one — what it is, what Rockstar showed, and what's still speculation.

01
Vice City
✅ Confirmed by Rockstar
Real-world inspiration: Miami, Florida

The primary urban environment and the anchor of the entire map. Vice City returns as a modern coastal metropolis — Art Deco architecture, neon-soaked waterways, high-rise skyline, and the iconic beachfront that defined the original. Rockstar has shown Vice City extensively across both trailers and the official screenshot release. It is the most detailed urban environment the studio has ever built. The city sits on the southeastern coast of Leonida and serves as the narrative home base for Jason and Lucia.

02
Leonida Keys
✅ Confirmed by Rockstar
Real-world inspiration: Florida Keys

A chain of island communities stretching south of Vice City. Rockstar has confirmed this is where the story of GTA6 begins — Lucia's backstory is rooted in the Keys before the narrative moves north into Vice City proper. Official screenshots show tropical water, island bridges, fishing communities, and a distinctly different pace and aesthetic from the urban core. The Keys represent the quieter, more isolated side of Leonida before everything goes sideways.

03
Grassrivers
✅ Confirmed by Rockstar
Real-world inspiration: Florida Everglades

Rockstar's version of the Everglades — swamps, subtropical wilderness, alligator-infested waterways, and dense mangrove forest. Official trailer footage showed police chases through Grassrivers terrain, airboat sequences, and the kind of wild, untamed environment that will make traversal genuinely different from anything in the franchise before. Rockstar confirmed alligators, snakes, raccoons, and multiple other wildlife species throughout this region. This is GTA6's wilderness zone.

04
Port Gellhorn
✅ Confirmed by Rockstar
Real-world inspiration: Port Everglades / Industrial Florida

A working port and industrial area on the eastern coast of Leonida. Where Vice City is glamour and Grassrivers is wilderness, Port Gellhorn is the grind — working-class neighborhoods, shipping infrastructure, industrial zones, and the economic machinery that keeps Leonida running. Official screenshots showed the port's waterfront, cargo operations, and the kind of gritty street-level environment that historically produces the best mission design in GTA games. This is where the criminal economy lives.

05
Ambrosia
✅ Confirmed by Rockstar
Real-world inspiration: Central Florida / Inland communities

The darker inland counterpart to Vice City's coastal glamour. Official Rockstar content described Ambrosia as featuring crime-ridden streets, abandoned tourist attractions, and decaying infrastructure — the Florida that doesn't make the postcards. Think sun-bleached strip malls, roadside motels with neon signs half burned out, and communities that the economic boom passed by. Ambrosia is where Leonida's social inequality is most visible, and historically that's exactly where the most interesting GTA storytelling happens.

06
Mount Kalaga
✅ Confirmed by Rockstar
Real-world inspiration: No direct Florida equivalent — creative liberty

The most visually dramatic region of the map. Mount Kalaga is Leonida's mountain — national park territory, forests, elevation changes, and the kind of terrain that doesn't exist in real Florida but gives GTA6 its vertical dimension. Official screenshots showed dense forest, mountain trails, and sweeping views across the state from elevation. This is where Rockstar breaks from the Florida inspiration to build something that serves gameplay — high-speed mountain chases, remote hideouts, and a wilderness that feels genuinely remote from Vice City's neon.

"Rockstar's official GTA VI page lists six named regions. Every one of them serves a distinct gameplay purpose. This is not a map — it's a world designed to give players a reason to go everywhere."

What The Scale Actually Means

Multiple community analysis projects have compared GTA6's confirmed map footage against GTA5's San Andreas and estimated Leonida is between 50-70% larger. Rockstar has not officially confirmed a size figure. What they have confirmed is that this is their most ambitious open world ever built — a statement they have never made lightly.

Six distinct regions with six completely different environmental identities means six different driving experiences, six different NPC ecosystems, six different mission design philosophies, and six different reasons to explore. GTA5 had the city, the desert, and the mountains. GTA6 has urban coastal, island chains, everglades wilderness, industrial port, decayed inland, and dramatic mountain terrain. The variety is not just visual — it changes how the game plays in every region.

What's Still Speculation

Fan maps showing specific road layouts, precise city boundaries, named towns within regions, and exact map dimensions are community projects — not Rockstar confirmations. Some are built from careful trailer frame analysis and are likely directionally accurate. None of them are official.

Specific locations like Sundown, Hillcrest, Crown County, Spring Garden, and others appearing on community maps come from trailer analysis and datamined information — credible but not officially confirmed by Rockstar. We tag those as ~ (credible leak) not ✅ (confirmed). The six regions above are the only ones Rockstar has officially named and described.

Bottom Line

Six regions. All confirmed. All distinct. All serving a different purpose in what Rockstar has built as their most ambitious world. November 19 is 173 days away. The map you'll be driving through on launch night covers Vice City's coastline, the Keys' island chains, Grassrivers' swamp wilderness, Port Gellhorn's industrial docks, Ambrosia's decayed inland towns, and Mount Kalaga's forest highlands. That's not a city. That's a state. And we get to drive all of it.
