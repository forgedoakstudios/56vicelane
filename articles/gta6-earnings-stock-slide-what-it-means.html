Take-Two Stock Slides 5% After Earnings — What It Actually Means For GTA6 Fans

📅 May 22, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane Staff 📊 Earnings · TTWO · GTA6

Numbers beat expectations. The stock dropped anyway. If you opened your finance app this morning and saw Take-Two down 5% after what looked like a solid earnings report, you probably had one question: is GTA6 still locked for November 19?

Yes. Completely. The stock slide has nothing to do with the game. Here's the full breakdown of what actually happened on the May 21 call — and what it means for every player waiting on November 19.

The Numbers That Beat

GTA Online still pulling weight

GTA Online, mobile titles, and Red Dead Redemption drove the outperformance. Revenue for the full fiscal year grew 18% to $6.66 billion. By every measure that matters for a company six months from the biggest game launch in history, the business is in strong shape.

🟢 November 19, 2026 confirmed on the call by CEO Strauss Zelnick. No delays. No caveats. The date is locked.

So Why Did The Stock Drop?

Wall Street doesn't trade on what happened — it trades on what's coming. And Take-Two's guidance for the new fiscal year came in below what analysts had penciled in.

The company guided for FY2027 net bookings of $8.0–8.2 billion. Analysts had been modeling closer to $9.4 billion. That gap — roughly $1.2 billion — is what sent the stock down 5.5% to around $225.

The softer guidance reflects two things: a cooling mobile market, and Take-Two's well-documented habit of setting conservative floor targets in years with major launches. They did the same thing before GTA5. They sandbag, then beat.

"Intentional conservatism, consistent with Take-Two's habit of setting floor targets during years marked by major launches." — Wedbush Securities

What The Analysts Said

The analyst community largely shrugged off the guidance miss. Most maintained bullish ratings and kept price targets well above current levels.

Seven analysts. Six bullish. The lone neutral voice is MoffettNathanson at $196, still 13 dollars below where the stock is trading today. The consensus is clear: most Wall Street money believes the GTA6 launch will drive significant upside from current levels.

The GTA Online Angle Nobody Is Talking About

Something significant came out of this call that matters directly to the Last Drive community. GTA Online's recurring spending grew 7% in a quarter where millions of players are clearly still logging in, still building crews, still running missions — thirteen years after the game launched.

That number tells you something real about what GTA5 means to people. This isn't a dead game kept on life support. Millions of players are still actively spending money inside it, right now, six months before its replacement drops.

That's the community we're saying goodbye to on November 18. That's the car culture, the cruise lobbies, the random moments that will never exist in exactly the same way again. The $7 earnings beat tells you the world isn't ready to let it go either.

No Price Reveal

One thing fans were hoping for — GTA6's price — did not come on the call. Insider Gaming confirmed before the call that pricing would not be disclosed, and that held. No official word on whether the game will launch at $70, $80, or higher.

That conversation is coming. Just not yet.

The Bottom Line For November 19

The stock is down. The game is on schedule. Those two facts can exist at the same time. Investors got spooked by guidance that came in below inflated expectations. The actual business beat. The CEO confirmed the date. The analysts who cover this company professionally are almost universally bullish.

If you were worried about a delay — don't be. If you were trying to understand why TTWO dropped the morning after a beat — now you know. November 19 is real. The Last Drive is November 18. Everything is on track.
