Take-Two Stock Slides 5% After Earnings — What It Actually Means For GTA6 Fans

May 22, 2026  •  56ViceLane Staff  •  Earnings · TTWO · GTA6

Numbers beat expectations. The stock dropped anyway. If you opened your finance app this morning and saw Take-Two down 5%, you probably had one question: is GTA6 still on for November 19?

Yes. Completely. The stock slide has nothing to do with the game. Here is the full breakdown.

GTA Online still pulling weight

GTA Online, mobile titles, and Red Dead Redemption drove the outperformance. Revenue for the full fiscal year grew 18% to $6.66 billion. By every measure that matters, the business is in strong shape.

November 19, 2026 confirmed on the call by CEO Strauss Zelnick. No delays. No caveats. The date is locked.

So Why Did The Stock Drop?

Wall Street trades on what is coming, not what happened. Take-Two guided for FY2027 net bookings of $8.0-8.2 billion. Analysts had been modeling closer to $9.4 billion. That gap sent the stock down 5.5% to around $225.

The softer guidance reflects a cooling mobile market and Take-Two's well-documented habit of setting conservative floor targets in years with major launches. They did the same thing before GTA5. They sandbag, then beat.

"Intentional conservatism, consistent with Take-Two's habit of setting floor targets during years marked by major launches." — Wedbush Securities

The GTA Online Angle Nobody Is Talking About

GTA Online recurring spending grew 7% in a quarter where millions of players are clearly still logging in thirteen years after launch. That number tells you something real about what GTA5 means to people. This is not a dead game on life support.

That is the community we are saying goodbye to on November 18. The Last Drive exists because of that number.

No Price Reveal

GTA6 pricing did not come on the call. Insider Gaming confirmed before the call that pricing would not be disclosed. No official word on whether the game launches at $70, $80, or higher. That conversation is coming — just not yet.

The Bottom Line

The stock is down. The game is on schedule. Those two facts can exist at the same time. Investors got spooked by guidance that came in below inflated expectations. The actual business beat. The CEO confirmed the date. November 19 is real. The Last Drive is November 18. Everything is on track.
