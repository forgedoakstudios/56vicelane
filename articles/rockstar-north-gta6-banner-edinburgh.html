Rockstar North Just Replaced Red Dead With A GTA6 Banner — It's Really Happening

56ViceLane · Original Art

How we source: ✅ Confirmed ~ Credible / community sourced ! Correction

A cherry picker parked outside Rockstar North's Edinburgh headquarters ~ is in the process of taking down the studio's Red Dead Redemption mural and replacing it with GTA6 artwork. Photos circulating on X show the work in progress. The studio that built Grand Theft Auto 6 ✅ is putting it on the outside of their own building — 172 days before launch on November 19, 2026 ✅. If you needed a sign that this is really happening, they just hung one.

What The Photos Show

Source: photos circulating on X (community sourced) ~ — unverified by Rockstar but widely reported

Images shared on X show a cherry picker platform lifted against the facade of the Rockstar North building in Edinburgh, Scotland ~. The existing Red Dead Redemption mural — which has decorated the building's exterior — is visibly being removed. The GTA6 banner going up in its place has not yet been fully revealed in the photos, but the transition itself is clear. The building is changing its face, and it's changing it to GTA6.

Rockstar North ✅ is the primary development studio behind Grand Theft Auto 6. This isn't a billboard on a random building — this is the studio where the game was built, where the developers who made it go to work every day, decorating their own front door. That's a different kind of signal than a marketing campaign. It's the team itself saying: this is our game, and it's coming.

Red Dead Out. GTA6 In. What That Transition Means.

Source: confirmed studio history ✅; analysis ~

Rockstar North has had the Red Dead Redemption mural on their building since RDR2 — a game that launched in 2018 and remains one of the most acclaimed open-world titles ever made ✅. That mural has been there for years, through the entirety of GTA6's development cycle. The decision to take it down now — in the summer before the November 19 launch ✅ — is deliberate timing. The studio is transitioning its own identity from the last big thing to the next big thing, and they're doing it publicly.

For players, there's something poetic about it. Red Dead Redemption 2 was the last major release from Rockstar before GTA6 — and here, physically, it's being replaced. The wall that held Red Dead is now holding GTA6. The studio that spent years quietly building the most anticipated game in history is now loud about it. The banner isn't going up on someone else's building. It's going up on theirs ~.

The Marketing Push Is Starting

Source: Zelnick confirmed summer marketing push ✅; studio banner is consistent with that timeline ~

Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick confirmed earlier this year that the GTA6 marketing campaign would ramp up significantly in the back half of summer 2026 ✅. The Rockstar North banner is the first visible physical sign of that push beginning. If the studio's own building is getting the GTA6 treatment, the broader campaign — trailers, press, retail displays, platform integrations — is close behind ~.

It also fits the no-review-copies posture ✅ Rockstar has already committed to. They're not building anticipation through critic access — they're building it through presence. A massive banner on their own studio in Edinburgh is exactly that kind of move: visible, confident, on their terms. You don't put your game on your building unless you believe in what's inside it.

A cherry picker at Rockstar North's Edinburgh studio taking down Red Dead and putting GTA6 up isn't a subtle signal. It's the loudest thing a development studio can do without saying a word: we're done with what came before, and what comes next is on the wall.
