Take-Two Is Shutting Down RAGE:MP — And It's Not Really About GTA 5

May 28, 2026  ·  56ViceLane News Desk  ·  5 min read

Take-Two just hit RAGE:MP with a cease-and-desist order. One of GTA 5's biggest multiplayer modding platforms is shutting down — public servers gone June 1, everything gone August 31. Every other outlet is covering this as a GTA 5 story. It isn't. This is about GTA 6 Online and who controls the ecosystem when it launches.

May 25, 2026
C&D Issued — No New Servers
Take-Two and Rockstar issue cease-and-desist to RAGE:MP. No new community servers can go live immediately. Alt:V had already shut down in February.
June 1, 2026
Public Server Listing Goes Dark
The master public server list is permanently turned off. Players can no longer browse available servers.
Aug 31, 2026
Complete Shutdown
Game client, server toolkit, and all backend infrastructure permanently deactivated. RAGE:MP is done.
Nov 19, 2026
GTA 6 Launches
With the modding ecosystem fully consolidated under FiveM — which Rockstar owns — GTA 6 Online launches into a clean, controlled environment.

What Actually Happened

On May 25 the RAGE:MP administration team published a statement confirming they had received a cease-and-desist order from Take-Two Interactive. The message was unambiguous: "Rockstar Games and Take-Two Interactive have made it clear that FiveM is the only authorized platform for GTA V multiplayer modding, as defined in their Platform License Agreement."

RAGE:MP had been one of the largest independent multiplayer modding platforms for GTA 5 — supporting a massive roleplay community with custom servers, custom gameplay systems, and years of community-built content. It joins Alt:V, which shut down in February 2026, as the second major independent GTA modding platform eliminated this year.

With RAGE:MP gone, FiveM is now the only real way to play GTA roleplay on PC. And Rockstar owns FiveM.

The FiveM Backstory You Need To Know

This story has a remarkable arc. In 2015 Take-Two tried to shut FiveM down — the same company that now owns it. FiveM was a fan-made multiplayer mod that Rockstar originally opposed as a violation of their terms of service.

Then something changed. Rockstar watched GTA roleplay servers on FiveM turn into a cultural phenomenon — millions of players, massive streaming audiences, and direct correlation to GTA 5 sales continuing years after launch. The game sold over 200 million copies partly because FiveM kept it alive and relevant.

So in 2023 Take-Two acquired Cfx.re, the company behind FiveM, bringing the entire operation in-house. The fans who built what they tried to kill were now Rockstar employees.

The Pattern Is Clear

Alt:V shut down in February 2026. RAGE:MP receives C&D in May 2026. Both eliminated in the same year GTA 6 launches. This is not coincidental cleanup. Take-Two is systematically removing every competitor to FiveM before GTA 6 Online debuts. They want one platform, one ecosystem, one revenue stream — and they own all of it.

Why This Is Really A GTA 6 Story

Every other outlet is covering RAGE:MP's shutdown as a sad day for the GTA 5 modding community. That framing misses the point entirely.

GTA 5 Online generated over $8 billion in revenue since 2013. The roleplay community on FiveM was a significant driver of that longevity. Take-Two has watched this model work for thirteen years. They are not going to leave GTA 6 Online's ecosystem fragmented across competing platforms.

By eliminating RAGE:MP and Alt:V before GTA 6 launches, Take-Two achieves several things simultaneously:

  • One platform, full control — every roleplay server, every custom experience, every creator runs through FiveM — which Rockstar owns and can monetize
  • Clean launch environment — no competing platforms fragmenting the GTA 6 Online player base on day one
  • Creator pipeline — the best server builders and community creators are being pushed toward a platform Rockstar controls, making them easier to partner with, feature, and potentially monetize
  • Revenue standardization — FiveM already has a creator monetization system. Consolidating the ecosystem means all of that money flows through Rockstar's infrastructure

What This Means For GTA 6 Online Players

If you play GTA 5 on a RAGE:MP server you have until August 31 to migrate or lose everything. The RAGE:MP team has pledged to help with migration to FiveM and says the platform will assist technically during the transition.

For GTA 6 players — expect FiveM to be deeply integrated into the GTA 6 Online ecosystem from day one. Rockstar didn't buy the company that built the best GTA multiplayer experience in history to ignore it. The technology, the team, and the community infrastructure that FiveM represents will almost certainly shape how GTA 6 Online handles player-created content and experiences.

The modding community didn't lose today. They got consolidated. There's a difference — and where that leads for GTA 6 is the story worth watching.
