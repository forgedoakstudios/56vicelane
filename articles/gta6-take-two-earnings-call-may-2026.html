Take-Two Earnings: GTA6 Nov 19 Locked, $8.2B Forecast — Full Breakdown

Take-Two posted record $6.72B in FY2026 net bookings and projected $8.0–8.2B for FY2027. GTA6 confirmed November 19. No pre-orders. No price. Here is everything that was said.

May 21, 2026  •  56ViceLane Staff  •  Earnings · TTWO · GTA6

Take-Two Interactive's Q4 fiscal 2026 earnings call was one of the most watched in gaming history — not because of the financial results, but because of what CEO Strauss Zelnick would say about GTA6 with 181 days until November 19 launch. Here is the complete breakdown of every announcement.

The Numbers

Q4 Revenue
$1.68B
Beat guidance of $1.51–1.56B
Full Year Net Bookings
$6.72B
Up 19% year over year
Non-GAAP EPS Q4
$0.80
Analysts expected $0.56
FY2027 Guidance
$8.0–8.2B
vs analyst estimate of $9.4B

Every metric beat expectations. The FY2027 guidance came in below analyst models, which sent the stock down 5.5% to around $225 after hours. That gap is the story of the day financially — but it does not affect GTA6 or its launch date in any way.

What Was Confirmed

✓ ConfirmedNovember 19, 2026 — Locked
Zelnick confirmed November 19 multiple times throughout the call. His exact words: "I think reiterating November 19 as a launch day today is probably a positive. I think we've been really clear that we're releasing the title on November 19." No hedging. No softening. The date is locked.
💰 Financial$8.0–8.2B FY2027 Forecast — GTA6 Is The Engine
The entire FY2027 financial forecast is built around GTA6. The GTA series accounts for 65% of Take-Two's projected net bookings. Zelnick confirmed GTA Online will continue as a separate live service alongside GTA6, though he declined to speculate on what GTA6 Online would look like at launch.
✓ ConfirmedMarketing Starts End of June 2026
Zelnick confirmed in a Bloomberg interview following the call that GTA6 marketing begins at the end of June 2026 — after the FIFA World Cup. Pre-orders go live when marketing starts. That means pre-orders in late June or July at the earliest.
✓ ConfirmedGTA5 Has Sold Close To 230 Million Copies
Zelnick confirmed GTA5 has now sold close to 230 million copies worldwide. Red Dead Redemption 2 has sold 85 million copies and is now the third best-selling game of all time. Both numbers underscore the scale of what November 19 represents.
✓ ConfirmedMillions Still Playing GTA Online Right Now
Zelnick referenced GTA Online as a "legacy game" with millions of active players — recurring spending grew 7% in Q4. Thirteen years after launch, the community is still there. That is exactly who The Last Drive is for.

What Was NOT Announced

Not AnnouncedPre-Orders
No pre-order date given on the call. Zelnick confirmed they will open when marketing begins — end of June at the earliest.
Not AnnouncedPrice
No official price confirmed. Zelnick defended $70 as a reasonable price point for games generally but confirmed nothing specific for GTA6. Dutch retailer leaks of $99–$115 remain unconfirmed.
Not AnnouncedTrailer 3
No new trailer announced on the call. Marketing begins end of June — a new trailer is likely to accompany that campaign launch.
Not AnnouncedPC Version Date
PC version was not addressed. November 19 console launch only confirmed. PC timing remains unannounced.

Why The Stock Dropped

The $8.0–8.2B guidance came in roughly $1.2B below what analysts had modeled. The miss reflects two things: a cooling mobile market, and Take-Two's consistent practice of setting conservative floor targets in years with major launches. Wedbush called it "intentional conservatism" and maintained their $300 price target.

Six of seven major analysts covering TTWO maintained bullish ratings after the call. The stock drop is a Wall Street reaction to guidance math, not a signal about the game.

Bottom Line

GTA6 is on schedule. November 19 is locked. Marketing starts end of June. Pre-orders open with marketing. The financial story is separate from the game story. Nothing from this call changes what happens on November 18 or 19.
