The Hacker Who Leaked GTA6 Is Out Of The Hospital — His Retrial Lands The Same Month As The Game

📅 July 16, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 6 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

The most infamous leak in Rockstar's history is heading back to court — in the same month GTA6 finally launches. Arion Kurtaj, the teenage hacker behind the September 2022 breach that leaked over 90 videos and screenshots of an early GTA6 build, has reportedly been released from the secure hospital where he'd been held since late 2023, and moved to a conventional prison to await a full criminal retrial.

The news was confirmed by BBC cybersecurity journalist Joe Tidy via Bluesky, and has since been picked up across gaming outlets including GamesRadar and TheGamer. According to that reporting, Kurtaj has been deemed medically fit to stand trial and will face a conventional criminal trial in November 2026 — the same month Grand Theft Auto VI ships to players worldwide on November 19.

ℹ️ Quick Recap: How We Got Here

In September 2022, a hacker breached Rockstar Games' internal systems and leaked more than 90 clips and screenshots of an in-development build of GTA6 — years before the game's official reveal. The leak is widely regarded as one of the most damaging breaches in gaming history. The hacker was later identified as a teenage member of the Lapsus$ hacking group.

A Hack Pulled Off From A Hotel Room

What made the original breach so remarkable wasn't just its scale — it was how it happened. Kurtaj, a member of the loosely organized hacking collective Lapsus$, is alleged to have carried out the Rockstar breach from a hotel room, using nothing more than an Amazon Fire Stick, a mobile phone, and the hotel room's TV. He was reportedly out on bail for a separate hacking case at the time, staying at the hotel as part of police-monitored bail conditions — and used the setup to breach one of the most valuable unreleased intellectual properties in the entertainment industry.

Rockstar has previously told the court the breach cost the company $5 million to recover from, along with "thousands of hours of staff time" spent on incident response, security remediation, and damage control across a leak that spread rapidly once the clips hit the open internet.

Why He Wasn't Tried The First Time

Kurtaj was never convicted in a conventional criminal trial for the Rockstar breach. In July 2023, he was deemed psychologically unfit to stand trial, tied to a severe autism diagnosis that the court determined prevented him from meaningfully participating in his own defense. Rather than a standard verdict, the case proceeded to what's known in the UK legal system as a "trial of the facts" — a process used to determine whether someone committed the acts in question without formally convicting them, when they've been ruled unfit to stand a normal trial.

By December 2023, the outcome of that process was that Kurtaj would be confined to a secure hospital indefinitely — reporting at the time cited concerns about his "intent to return to cybercrime as soon as possible" as a factor in that decision, rather than a fixed sentence with a release date.

What Changes This Time

The key detail in this week's reporting is that Kurtaj has now been assessed as medically fit to stand trial — the specific bar he failed to clear back in 2023. That reassessment is what's clearing the way for a conventional criminal trial rather than another "trial of the facts" process. It's a meaningful legal shift: a full trial means an actual verdict is possible this time, rather than the hospital-order outcome from three years ago.

The timing is the part that's impossible to ignore. A criminal trial connected directly to the most notorious leak in GTA6's development history is set to unfold in the same month the game itself finally reaches players. For a story that's been part of the GTA6 news cycle since before the game was even officially announced, that's a strange kind of symmetry — the leak that defined years of speculation about the game, resolving in court right as the speculation finally ends.

⚠️ What's Still Unconfirmed

The exact November trial date, and the full legal basis for Kurtaj's transfer out of the secure hospital, are based on reporting citing BBC journalist Joe Tidy and subsequent gaming press coverage — not a direct court filing or official statement reviewed by 56ViceLane. Details of ongoing UK criminal proceedings can also shift as court schedules move. Treat the November timing as reported, not guaranteed.

Why This Story Still Matters To GTA6 Coverage

It's easy to treat this as old news resurfacing — the leak itself is nearly four years old at this point. But the case has never actually been resolved, and Rockstar's own account of the damage ($5 million, thousands of staff hours) makes clear the company still treats it as a serious matter rather than a closed chapter. A trial landing the same month as launch also means gaming press attention on the case is likely to spike right alongside GTA6 launch coverage — this may not be the last update on the Kurtaj case before November 19.

Whatever the outcome of the trial, it won't change what ships on November 19. But for a leak that shaped years of community speculation, theory-crafting, and leaked-footage analysis long before any official trailer existed, seeing the legal chapter finally move toward a real resolution — in the same month the game it leaked finally arrives — is the kind of detail that's hard not to notice.
