Does GTA 6 Need PS Plus? Will It Be Always Online? Everything We Actually Know

May 28, 2026  ·  56ViceLane News Desk  ·  6 min read

Three questions every GTA 6 buyer is asking right now. Rockstar hasn't answered any of them. Here's what we know, what we can reasonably expect, what remains genuinely unknown — and one thought at the end that we can't stop thinking about.

❓ Question 1 — Do You Need PlayStation Plus To Play GTA 6?

The short answer is: probably yes for online, definitely no for single player — but Rockstar hasn't confirmed anything yet.

Here's how it works with GTA 5 right now. Single player — no PS Plus required. You can play the entire story campaign offline without a subscription. GTA Online — PS Plus required on PS5. You need an active subscription to access multiplayer.

There is no reason to believe GTA 6 will change this model. Sony has structured PS Plus around online multiplayer access for years. Rockstar has never made their single player games require a subscription. The most likely scenario is identical to GTA 5 — single player is free to play without PS Plus, online requires it.

The only wildcard is if Sony and Rockstar announce something unusual as part of their marketing deal — like a PS Plus free trial period or a bundled subscription offer with GTA 6 pre-orders. That would be unprecedented but not impossible given the scale of this launch.

Most Likely: PS Plus required for online only — not for single player
❓ Question 2 — Will GTA 6 Require A Constant Internet Connection?

This is the "always online" question — and based on everything Rockstar has ever done, the answer is almost certainly no for single player.

GTA 5's single player runs completely offline. Red Dead Redemption 2's single player runs completely offline. Rockstar has never shipped a single player game that requires a persistent internet connection. Their track record on this is consistent across every title for over a decade.

Where internet becomes relevant is GTA 6 Online — multiplayer requires a connection by definition. But the idea that you couldn't play Jason and Lucia's story campaign without being connected to Rockstar's servers has no basis in anything they've said or done.

The community fear around always-online comes from other publishers — Ubisoft, EA, and others who have shipped single player games with online requirements. Rockstar is not those publishers. They have never done this and there is no signal they plan to start.

Almost Certainly No — Rockstar has never required always-online for single player
❓ Question 3 — Is GTA 6 Primarily An Online Game?

This is the most complex question of the three — and the one Rockstar has been most deliberately vague about.

What we know: The trailers show an extensive single player story featuring Jason and Lucia across a massive open world version of Vice City. The production values are extraordinary. This is not a game that was built as a wrapper for online play.

What we also know: GTA Online generated over $8 billion in revenue and kept GTA 5 alive for thirteen years. Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick has repeatedly pointed to online as the long-term revenue engine. The RAGE:MP shutdown, the FiveM acquisition, and the systematic consolidation of the modding ecosystem all point to heavy investment in GTA 6 Online infrastructure.

The honest answer is that GTA 6 is both — a massive single player story AND a platform for online play that Rockstar expects to generate revenue for the next decade. The question of which gets more attention after launch depends entirely on how much Rockstar has learned from GTA 5's trajectory.

What Zelnick calling GTA Online a "legacy game" actually signals is that GTA 6 Online is something bigger and different — not a copy of what came before. The details of what that means remain unknown.

Unknown — Both single player and online are confirmed, scale and focus TBD

The One Thing Nobody Is Asking

Everyone is focused on the questions above. But there's a bigger, weirder question sitting underneath all of them that Rockstar hasn't touched with a ten-foot pole.

You've spent thirteen years in GTA Online. You have properties. Cars. A criminal empire worth tens of millions. A character you've built from scratch. GTA Online has over 100 million registered players. Some of those players have hundreds of hours invested.

What happens to all of that when GTA 6 launches?

Rockstar hasn't said. Not one word. And given that they just called GTA Online a "legacy game" — that silence is either very reassuring or very ominous depending on how you read it.

One Last Thought Before You Go...

Maybe we should be wondering what it might look like if GTA V Online somehow merged into GTA 6 Online. 🤔

Now that's a thought. Lol.

Just speculation. That's all.

The Bottom Line

PS Plus — probably required for online, not for single player. Always online — almost certainly no for story mode. Online-only game — no, but online will be massive. And what happens to GTA 5 Online when GTA 6 launches? We're watching. You'll know when we know.
