Even Assassin's Creed Is Bracing for GTA6 — Dev Begs Fans "Please Play Us" Post-Launch

📅 July 20, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 6 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link
✅ Confirmed

This one isn't a leak or a theory — it's a rival studio's own director, on the record, admitting they're planning around GTA6's launch window. Direct quotes below, sourced from GamesRadar.

Every so often, a story comes along that says more about GTA6's shadow than anything Rockstar itself has published. This is one of those stories, and it didn't even come from Rockstar. It came from Ubisoft.

Richard Knight, the director behind Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced, sat down for an interview about the game's future and ended up doing something no games-industry director usually does out loud: he named the competition, by implication, and asked players for mercy.

"Please play us when GTA 6 comes out," Knight said, according to the original report. He wasn't joking, or at least not entirely. He was teasing genuine post-launch support plans for Black Flag Resynced — content designed, in part, to give players a reason to stick around once November 19 arrives and every other game on the planet becomes background noise.

What Knight Actually Said

Asked directly whether Ubisoft Singapore has official post-launch support plans for Black Flag Resynced, Knight didn't confirm anything on the record — but he didn't deny it either. "I can't say anything officially," he said, before immediately undercutting the non-answer: continued support "doesn't feel like a crazy bet," he added, because "we definitely need to support the game."

That's about as close to a confirmation as a director can get without technically confirming anything. Read between the lines and the message is clear: more content is coming, and it's coming with one eye on the calendar.

Some of what's already been teased or planned, according to the same report:

  • A New Game Plus mode — giving completionists and returning players a reason to run the game back
  • A toggle to hide Edward's blow pipe — a small quality-of-life change, but the kind of detail that signals a team still actively polishing the game
  • The catch ledge mechanic from Assassin's Creed Shadows, potentially being folded into Black Flag Resynced's traversal
  • Possible side objectives added to existing missions, though Knight was clear this part isn't locked in

Why This Is a GTA6 Story, Not Just an Assassin's Creed Story

Here's the part that matters for us. Knight didn't say "we're adding content because our players asked for it" or "because we have a roadmap." He said it in direct reference to GTA6's release. That's a studio explicitly acknowledging that a competitor's launch is a survival event for their own player retention — and building features specifically to soften the blow.

This is not a small thing. Triple-A studios don't usually say the quiet part out loud about a rival's game eating their playerbase. They usually just quietly schedule their own big content drops around it, or avoid the topic in interviews entirely. Knight didn't avoid it. He leaned into it, half as a joke and half as an actual strategy briefing.

ℹ️ Good To Know

GTA5, a game that came out in 2013, is still routinely one of the best-selling titles in the world every single year through GTA Online. GTA6 launching isn't just "a new game drops" — for the entire industry, it's closer to a scheduling event on the level of a Super Bowl. Studios plan around it the way retailers plan around Black Friday.

The Bigger Pattern

Black Flag Resynced won't be the last game whose devs quietly (or not so quietly) brace for the GTA6 window. Expect more of this in the coming months — delayed release dates that suspiciously avoid mid-November, "content roadmap" announcements timed to launch right before or right after, and more interviews where a director says some version of "we know what's coming, and we're planning for it."

It's a strange kind of compliment. An entire industry, adjusting its calendar around one game that hasn't even launched yet.

Our Take

Say what you want about live-service anxiety in this industry, but you have to respect the honesty here. Most studios would rather eat glass than admit a competitor's launch date is a threat to their own numbers. Knight just said it plainly, with a bit of humor, and the internet noticed.

So — good luck to Black Flag Resynced. Genuinely. We hope the New Game Plus mode is great. But let's be honest with each other: come November 19, most of us are going to be in Vice City.
