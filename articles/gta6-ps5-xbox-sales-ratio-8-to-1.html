GTA6 Is Selling 8x More Copies On PS5 Than Xbox — And That Number Is Wild

📅 June 29, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 8 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link
⚠️ Heads Up

The 8:1 PS5-to-Xbox sales ratio figure is an unverified claim circulating via third-party reporting. Microsoft has explicitly stated it does not represent their pre-order data. Treat the specific number as unconfirmed — the broader trend of PS5 dominance in GTA6 pre-orders may well be real, but the ratio itself has not been independently verified. Confirmed pre-order pricing figures and platform availability are noted separately below.

Pre-orders for GTA6 went live on June 25, 2026 — and within days, a number surfaced that set gaming Twitter on fire. According to circulating reports, GTA6 is selling approximately eight times more copies on PS5 than on Xbox Series X|S. Eight to one. A ratio so lopsided it sounds like a misprint. Microsoft moved quickly to contest it. And now the entire console war debate — long simmering in the background of the GTA6 hype cycle — has exploded into the foreground.

Whether or not the precise ratio holds up to scrutiny, the conversation it has sparked is very real. We're going to break down where the claim came from, what Microsoft actually said, what the confirmed pre-order landscape looks like, and what any of this means for the biggest game launch of 2026.

Where The 8:1 Claim Came From

The figure emerged via industry tracking reports, with NewsNow among the outlets surfacing the claim. Third-party sales trackers — the same outfits that have historically monitored physical retail and digital storefront trends — reportedly flagged an extreme imbalance in GTA6 pre-order momentum favouring PlayStation heavily over Xbox.

Eight to one is not a number that comes out of nowhere. It's specific enough to feel like it came from actual data. But "specific" doesn't mean verified, and that's a crucial distinction here. Sales tracking firms work with retailer samples, panel data, and extrapolation — not direct access to platform holder dashboards. The methodology behind a ratio like this matters enormously, and those details have not been made fully public.

What we do know is that the claim gained traction fast. Within hours of surfacing, the 8:1 figure was being shared across Reddit, Twitter, and gaming forums as if it were settled fact. That velocity tells you something about the gaming community's appetite for console war content — even when the data behind the headline is genuinely uncertain.

ℹ️ Good To Know

Third-party sales trackers like GSD (Games Sales Data) in Europe and NPD in the US do not have direct access to digital storefront numbers from Sony or Microsoft. Their digital estimates are based on panel data and modelled projections. Physical retail figures are generally more reliable. Any ratio comparing PS5 vs. Xbox digital pre-orders should be treated as an estimate, not a platform-reported figure.

Microsoft's Response — And Why It Matters

Microsoft didn't stay quiet. The company pushed back on the claim directly, stating that the 8:1 figure "doesn't represent pre-order data" — a carefully worded denial that stops short of providing alternative numbers while clearly distancing the company from the reported ratio.

That response is interesting for a few reasons. First, it's defensive in a way that suggests the claim landed hard enough internally to require a public rebuttal. If the number were obviously wrong and easily dismissed, a company of Microsoft's size might simply ignore it. The fact that they responded suggests the conversation was reaching a scale they couldn't comfortably ignore.

Second, the phrasing "doesn't represent pre-order data" is notable for what it doesn't say. It doesn't claim PS5 and Xbox are neck-and-neck. It doesn't offer a counter-ratio. It challenges the specific figure while leaving the broader picture — that PS5 is likely outperforming Xbox on GTA6 pre-orders — entirely unchallenged.

Microsoft has spent years trying to close the installed base gap with PlayStation. The Xbox Series X|S installed base remains significantly smaller than the PS5's globally, which means any multiplatform title selling proportionally to installed base will naturally show PS5 ahead. An 8:1 ratio would imply Xbox is underperforming even relative to its smaller base — and that's the part Microsoft appears most motivated to contest.

The Pre-Order Landscape — What Is Actually Confirmed

Here's what we can say with confidence: GTA6 pre-orders opened June 25, 2026 across multiple storefronts simultaneously. The Rockstar Store, PlayStation Store, Xbox Store, Amazon, and GameStop all went live on the same day, with the following confirmed edition pricing.

Both editions launched at the same price across both platforms, so the pricing structure itself gives neither PlayStation nor Xbox a competitive advantage. If PS5 is outselling Xbox at the pre-order stage, the reason isn't price — it's platform preference, installed base, or both.

Why PS5 Dominating Makes Structural Sense

Even setting the disputed ratio aside entirely, there are strong structural reasons why PS5 would lead GTA6 pre-orders by a wide margin. It starts with the installed base. As of mid-2026, PlayStation 5 has sold significantly more units globally than Xbox Series X|S. Sony has consistently outpaced Microsoft in hardware sales this generation, with some estimates putting the gap at roughly 2:1 or higher in favour of PS5 worldwide.

But GTA6's pre-order dynamic may go beyond raw installed base proportions. Rockstar Games titles have historically skewed toward PlayStation in terms of the most passionate early adopters. The series launched on PlayStation consoles first during the PS2 era. The brand association runs deep in the PlayStation community. And PlayStation's marketing deal with Rockstar — which has been in place for multiple GTA cycles — means PS5 is the platform most associated with GTA6 in advertising.

The Marketing Deal Factor

Sony has a longstanding promotional partnership with Rockstar Games for the GTA franchise. This doesn't mean GTA6 is exclusive to PS5 — it absolutely isn't — but it does mean that the majority of co-branded advertising, promotional placement, and official gaming event showcases have featured the PS5 version prominently. When a casual gamer sees a GTA6 ad, they're more likely to see it positioned alongside PlayStation branding. That kind of sustained marketing association compounds over years and tilts pre-order intent toward the platform being spotlighted.

The Xbox Installed Base Problem

Microsoft's own hardware challenges are not a secret. Xbox Series X|S has struggled to match PS5 in unit sales throughout this generation. The reasons are complex — pricing at launch, lack of Xbox-exclusive system sellers, and a strategic pivot toward Game Pass and cloud gaming that has somewhat deprioritised console hardware as a growth driver.

This matters for GTA6 specifically because console launches and major title drops tend to amplify whatever platform momentum already exists. PS5 players buying GTA6 at launch will likely play on a console they already own and love. For some potential Xbox buyers, GTA6 may actually be one of the titles prompting them to buy a console in the first place — which is a different conversion path that takes longer and may show up in sales data later.

What Microsoft Is Actually Competing With

It's worth being clear about the full competitive picture here. Microsoft is not simply competing with Sony for GTA6 pre-orders. They're also competing with their own Game Pass ecosystem. A segment of Xbox players who might otherwise have bought GTA6 outright on day one could be waiting to see if the title eventually comes to Game Pass — even if no such announcement has been made and Rockstar has given no indication that's the plan.

Game Pass has been a transformative product for Microsoft, but it has also trained a portion of their user base to wait rather than buy. That behavioural shift could suppress Xbox pre-order numbers independent of any genuine console preference, and it would show up in sales tracking data in a way that makes the platform's GTA6 performance look worse than the underlying demand picture might actually warrant.

ℹ️ Good To Know

As of this writing, GTA6 has not been announced for Game Pass. There is no confirmed Game Pass day-one deal. Any Xbox player waiting on that basis is working from speculation, not confirmed information. If you want GTA6 on day one, pre-ordering is currently the only guaranteed path.

Does The Ratio Actually Matter?

Here's the honest answer: for most players, not really. Whether GTA6 is selling 8:1 or 3:1 or 5:1 in favour of PS5, the game ships on both platforms on November 19. Rockstar will not be pulling the Xbox version if PS5 pre-orders dominate — that's not how major multiplatform releases work. The game exists, the servers will be populated on both platforms, and the experience is designed to be equivalent on current-gen hardware.

What the ratio does matter for is the broader industry conversation about where the console market is heading. If GTA6 — the most anticipated title of the decade — shows a historically lopsided platform split, it becomes ammunition in a larger debate about whether Xbox's hardware strategy is sustainable. That's a conversation Microsoft is very motivated to shape, which explains the swift pushback.

It also matters for the GTA Online ecosystem that launches after the story mode. Server population, community size, and matchmaking quality will all be influenced by how many players are on each platform. A heavily PS5-weighted playerbase at launch would mean the richest GTA Online experience in Year One is almost certainly on PlayStation — not because the Xbox version is worse, but because population density drives the social experience of any live service game.

The Console War Nobody Wanted — But Here We Are

Nobody really asked for GTA6 to become the flashpoint for the 2026 console war. Rockstar certainly didn't position it that way. The game is a landmark release on its own terms — a decade-plus in development, the most expensive entertainment product ever made, a cultural event that transcends the gaming space.

But numbers create narratives. And when a number as stark as 8:1 enters the conversation, it's going to generate heat regardless of how contested the figure is. Microsoft's pushback kept the story alive for another news cycle. The gaming press amplified it. And now it's the conversation dominating the week that GTA6 pre-orders launched — which is not exactly the framing Rockstar or Take-Two were hoping for either.

What's clear is that PS5 players are showing up for GTA6 in force. The pre-order velocity on PlayStation storefronts is visibly high. Whether Xbox closes the gap between now and November 19 may depend on factors outside any one sales report — a hardware bundle, a Game Pass clarification, or simply a surge of new Xbox buyers motivated specifically by GTA6.

What To Watch Before Launch Day

The next few months before November 19 will tell us a lot. Here's what to track if you want to understand how this story develops:

  • Official pre-order milestones — Rockstar or Take-Two may announce pre-order records if the numbers are favourable. If they stay quiet, that's informative too.
  • Microsoft's hardware strategy — any Xbox Series X GTA6 bundle announcement would signal Microsoft recognising the gap and trying to close it with hardware incentives.
  • Retail chart data — UK and European physical retail charts tend to break down platform split more reliably than digital estimates. Watch for GTA6 entries as launch approaches.
  • Take-Two earnings calls — the company's Q3 and Q4 2026 calls will include commentary on pre-order performance. Executives won't give platform breakdown numbers, but language around "strong console performance" vs specifics is worth parsing.
  • Game Pass announcement (or lack thereof) — a confirmed Game Pass deal before launch would dramatically change Xbox pre-order behaviour overnight. The absence of an announcement by October should be taken as a signal that day-one Game Pass is not happening.
✅ Our Recommendation

If you're on the fence between platforms and GTA6 is the deciding factor: buy the console your friends are on. GTA Online in Year One will be driven heavily by social play, and population density matters. If your crew is on PS5, go PS5. If they're on Xbox, go Xbox. The game itself will be the same — the community around it won't be.

The Bigger Picture — November 19 Is Still The Only Date That Matters

Sales ratios, disputed figures, and Microsoft press statements are going to come and go between now and launch. The only number that will matter on November 19 is zero — as in, zero days until the game is in players' hands.

Rockstar Games has been building GTA6 for over a decade. The game does not care about sales tracking methodology or console war discourse. It will ship on both platforms and almost certainly break every commercial record in its path regardless of which side of the PS5/Xbox debate is ultimately right.

The 8:1 claim may be accurate, exaggerated, or somewhere in between. Microsoft may be right that the number doesn't represent their pre-order data. Both things can be true simultaneously — the figure can be contested and the underlying PS5 momentum can still be very real. That's where the evidence currently sits.

What is not in doubt: GTA6 pre-orders are open right now. The Standard Edition is priced at $79.99 and the Ultimate Edition at $99.99, available on both PS5 and Xbox Series X|S through the Rockstar Store, PlayStation Store, Xbox Store, Amazon, and GameStop. If you've been waiting, you don't need to wait any longer.

November 19. Both platforms. One game. The console war can sort itself out on the charts — you just need to decide where you're playing.
