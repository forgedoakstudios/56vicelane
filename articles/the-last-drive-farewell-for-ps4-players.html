PS4 Players Are Being Left Behind — Why November 18 Is the Last Night Everyone Plays Together

📅 July 4, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 11 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

PS4 players are being abandoned. 😭 You're gonna feel it.

GTA5 isn't going anywhere. GTA Online keeps getting new heists, new content, new reasons to log back in. But when GTA6 launches on PS5 and Xbox Series X|S only, the shared world millions of us have lived in for over a decade quietly splits in two — and everyone still on a PS4 gets left on the old side of that line. November 19 isn't just a release date. For a huge chunk of this community, it's the day the fanbase stops being one thing and starts being two.

What's Actually Confirmed

There's no ambiguity here, which is what makes this hit different than most GTA6 stories. Rockstar and Take-Two have been consistent and direct about platform support: GTA6 launches exclusively on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S. There is no PS4 or Xbox One version. A PC release isn't expected until roughly 2027, well after the console launch window. Meanwhile, GTA5 and GTA Online continue running as they always have, on every platform they currently support, with no announced end date.

The Math Nobody Wants To Say Out Loud

The PS4 has been in homes for over a decade. Millions of players who grew up on GTA5 never upgraded — not because they don't care about gaming, but because a console upgrade is a real financial decision, and plenty of people had zero reason to make it while GTA5 kept working fine on the hardware they already owned. Those same players are now staring down a launch where the biggest game of the generation simply doesn't run on their system, full stop. No patch fixes that. No workaround exists. It's a hardware wall.

That's not a small niche. It's a meaningful slice of the existing fanbase — friend groups, heist crews, longtime lobbies — where some players upgrade on day one and others simply can't, whether that's about cost, availability, or just not being ready to make the jump yet. The shared experience that's defined this community for over a decade starts fracturing the moment GTA6 goes live, because half the group can play it and half can't.

GTA5 Isn't Dying — But It's Not The Same Room Anymore

Here's the part that actually matters for anyone stuck on PS4: GTA5 Online isn't shutting down. Rockstar has given no indication it's pulling support, and the game has proven itself as close to evergreen as anything in this industry. That's genuinely good news — PS4 players aren't losing access to the world they've spent years in.

What they're losing is the sense that everyone's still in that world together. Right now, GTA5 Online is where the whole community overlaps — new-gen players, old-gen players, people who've been grinding since 2013 and people who joined last month. The moment GTA6 launches, attention shifts. Lobbies thin out. The crew that used to run heists together on Friday nights starts splitting time between two different games, and for players who can only access one of them, that split isn't a choice — it's made for them.

ℹ️ Good To Know

Nothing about GTA6 launching affects existing GTA5 saves, GTA+ subscriptions, or GTA Online progress. The games run in parallel — the change is in where the community's attention goes, not in what PS4 owners are able to keep playing.

Why November 18 Matters

This is exactly the gap The Last Drive exists to fill. The night before GTA6 launches, players across every platform — PS4 included — get one final night where the whole community is still standing in the same room. No new-gen exclusivity, no hardware wall, no split. Just everyone who's ever logged into Los Santos, out on the road together one more time before the fanbase quietly divides itself by console generation the next morning.

It's a small, deliberate thing against a genuinely big shift. GTA6 launching isn't a tragedy — it's the natural, confirmed next step for a franchise that's been building toward this for years. But for players who won't be there on day one because of hardware, not choice, having one last shared night before that gap opens up is worth marking on the calendar.

What PS4 Players Can Actually Do About It

  • Keep playing GTA5 Online. It's not going anywhere, and the community there isn't disappearing overnight — it's just going to feel different as attention shifts toward GTA6.
  • Plan the upgrade on your own timeline. A PS5 or Xbox Series X isn't required by launch day. There's no rush that outweighs a smart purchase decision.
  • Watch the PC window. If a console upgrade isn't in the cards, the ~2027 PC release is the more realistic path in for players who already have solid PC hardware.
  • Show up for The Last Drive. Whatever platform you're on, November 18 is built to include everyone — no exclusivity, no wall.
✅ Our Recommendation

If you're on PS4 and not upgrading right away, that's a completely reasonable call — GTA5 Online isn't going anywhere. But don't miss The Last Drive on November 18. However this launch shakes out for you personally, that night belongs to everyone who's ever been part of this — one last ride together before the room changes shape.
