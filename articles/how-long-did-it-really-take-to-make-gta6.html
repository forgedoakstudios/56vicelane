How Long Did It Really Take to Make GTA6?

📅 July 21, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 6 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

This one came out of a conversation in our own comments, and it's too good not to dig into properly: how long did it actually take Rockstar to make GTA6? The official record is public. The unofficial story fans have been passing around for over a decade is a lot more interesting — and a lot harder to pin down.

📋 The Confirmed Timeline

Strip out every rumor and here's what's actually on record, straight from Rockstar and Take-Two's own statements:

September 2013

GTA5 launches. Rockstar spends the next several years pouring almost all of its public-facing energy into GTA Online instead of a new mainline title — a live-service goldmine that, in hindsight, bought the studio years of runway to build the next game right instead of rushing it.

2019 (reported)

Gaming press first reports a major hiring surge at Rockstar studios worldwide, widely read at the time as the clearest public signal that a new mainline GTA was in serious production — though Rockstar itself said nothing official.

February 2022

First official confirmation. Take-Two confirms on an investor call that "active development" of the next Grand Theft Auto is underway — the first time the studio put it in writing.

September 2022

A major leak puts dozens of clips of early build footage online. Not how Rockstar wanted the reveal to go, but it made GTA6's existence undeniable to anyone still on the fence.

December 2023

Trailer 1 drops. Vice City, Leonida's tropical sprawl, and Lucia are officially real. The internet does not sleep that night.

May 2025

Trailer 2 lands, confirming more of the open world and giving fans their first extended look at Jason.

November 19, 2026

Launch day. After two delays from an original Fall 2025 target, this is where we land.

Counted from GTA5's launch to GTA6's, that's roughly thirteen years between mainline entries. For comparison, GTA4 to GTA5 was about five. That gap alone tells you something changed about how Rockstar builds games — bigger scope, next-gen tech from scratch, two protagonists instead of one, and a company that no longer needed to rush because GTA Online was printing money the whole time.

👂 The Rumor Nobody Can Fully Confirm

Here's the part that doesn't show up in any earnings call. For years, a specific claim has floated around GTA fan communities and leak forums: that conversations about "the next one" — what would become GTA6 — were happening inside Rockstar before GTA5 even shipped in September 2013.

It's not an outlandish idea. Big studios routinely start kicking around ideas for their next project while the current one is still in the oven — that's just how the industry works. But the specific claim that GTA6 ideation predates GTA5's launch has never been confirmed by Rockstar or Take-Two in any official capacity. It's the kind of thing that gets passed around for over a decade, picks up detail with every retelling, and never quite gets a source anyone can point to.

🤔 Rumor, Not Fact

We're flagging this clearly: the "before GTA5 launched" claim is community lore, not a confirmed Rockstar statement. We're not treating it as fact — but it's exactly the kind of long-running fan theory worth talking about openly instead of pretending it doesn't exist.

🧮 So, How Long Did It Take?

Depends which clock you're reading. By the only dates anyone can actually prove — hiring surges around 2019 through a November 2026 launch — that's around seven years of hard production. Stretch it to the official "active development" confirmation in 2022, and it's closer to four and a half. Believe the decade-plus rumor, and you're talking about the longest gestation period of any mainline GTA by a wide margin.

Whichever number you land on, one thing isn't up for debate: this is the most time Rockstar has ever spent between two mainline entries, and after 13 years of GTA Online holding the fort, November 19 is finally almost here.
