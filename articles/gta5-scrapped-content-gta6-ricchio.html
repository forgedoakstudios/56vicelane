Could Scrapped GTA5 Content Be Hiding in GTA6? A Former Rockstar Producer Weighs In

📅 July 21, 2026 ✍️ 56ViceLane News Desk ⏱️ 6 min read
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

Every long-running franchise leaves a graveyard behind it — missions that got cut, features that never shipped, whole ideas that ran out of runway before the disc got pressed. GTA5 was no exception. So when a long-time Rockstar developer floats the idea that some of that scrapped GTA5 material could end up in GTA6, of course the internet perks up. But read past the headline and the real answer is more grounded — and honestly, more interesting — than "your cut heist is coming back."

🎙️ Where this came from

The comments come from John Ricchio, a former Rockstar producer with real credits on the GTA era of the studio, speaking in a developer interview that Dexerto picked up and ran with under the headline that scrapped GTA5 content "might" be in GTA6. It's the same ex-producer who recently described the sheer scale of GTA6 as "monstrous," so he's been a steady source of inside-baseball takes on how Rockstar actually builds these things.

And here's the honest part, the part that doesn't fit neatly on a thumbnail: Ricchio isn't promising anything. He's answering a "could it happen" question like a developer, not a hype man — which is exactly why it's worth reading his actual words instead of just the headline.

🔧 "It depends on the tech"

Ricchio's core point is that reusing old content isn't a creative decision so much as a technical one. According to the interview, whether a cut mission or system can be carried forward comes down to how much the engine changed underneath it.

"It depends on how much the tech changes between games," he said. "If the tech is fundamentally the same, or similar enough, you can sometimes plop it in." The catch, of course, is that GTA6 is not running on similar-enough tech. By every account — including Ricchio's own "monstrous" comment — GTA6 is a generational leap in scope and technology over GTA5. When the foundation changes that much, old assets and old mission scripting don't just "plop in." They'd have to be rebuilt from scratch, at which point you're not really reusing anything.

🤔 The Reality Check

A 13-year gap between GTA5 and GTA6 means the engine, the tools, and the pipelines all moved on. "Reusing" a cut GTA5 mission in that environment usually means rebuilding it entirely — so even if the idea survives, the original content almost certainly doesn't.

🗄️ The precedent nobody talks about

Now, before you write the whole thing off — Ricchio did confirm that Rockstar has genuinely done this before. He pointed to a cancelled project earlier in the studio's history whose work didn't entirely die with it.

"There was a game that got cancelled at Rockstar," he explained, "and some of the missions that we had designed for that ended up in GTA4." That's the kind of detail that makes these developer interviews worth reading — a small, concrete confirmation that ideas at Rockstar don't always stay buried. Sometimes a mission designed for one game finds a second life in another. It's happened.

GTA5's cutting-room floor has fed years of fan theories. The question is whether any of it actually crosses over.

But — and this is the whole ballgame — Ricchio was quick to add that it's the exception, not the rule. "I don't think it's very common unless you're doing a fast-follow," he said, meaning a quick sequel built on the same tech within a short window. GTA6 is the polar opposite of a fast-follow. It's the longest gap between mainline GTA games ever.

💡 Studios aren't short on ideas

The most quietly revealing thing Ricchio said isn't about tech at all. It's about why studios reach for old content in the first place — and why they usually don't. In his words, "you're usually not limited by ideas."

That lands differently when you think about it. Fans tend to imagine a vault of brilliant cut content that a studio is dying to finally use. The reality Ricchio describes is the opposite: a team like Rockstar's has more good ideas than it can ever ship, so there's rarely a reason to go digging through the archives unless something was just really cool and worth resurrecting on its own merits. New game, new tech, new ambitions — the pull is almost always forward, not backward.

🧭 So what should you actually expect?

Put it all together and the honest read is this: the odds of literal, recognizable "scrapped GTA5 content" showing up in GTA6 are low. Not zero — Rockstar has a documented history of recycling a great idea when it fits — but low. What's far more likely is something subtler: a concept, a mission structure, a character beat that a designer loved back in the GTA5 days, rebuilt from the ground up for a new engine until it's GTA5 only in spirit.

And maybe that's the better outcome anyway. A cut GTA5 mission bolted into GTA6 would feel like a relic. A great old idea, rebuilt with 13 years of new tech behind it? That's just how good sequels get made.

📌 The Takeaway

Former Rockstar producer John Ricchio says reusing cut GTA5 content in GTA6 is technically possible but unlikely, mostly because the tech gap is so large — though he confirms Rockstar has repurposed cut missions before (some ended up in GTA4). Treat the "scrapped content returns" angle as a fun maybe, not a promise.

As reported by Dexerto. Quotes attributed to former Rockstar producer John Ricchio via his developer interview. This piece includes our own analysis of what those comments do and don't mean — clearly our read, not Rockstar's official word.
