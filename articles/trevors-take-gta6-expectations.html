Trevor's Take: Everybody Needs To Calm Down About GTA6

📅 July 24, 2026 ⏱️ 4 min read
Trevor
Unsolicited, As Always
📘 Facebook 𝕏 Twitter 🔴 Reddit 🔗 Copy Link

So the desk ran two very long, very thoughtful articles this week about whether GTA6 can live up to thirteen years of expectations. Real nice work. Charts. A "for and against" section. Somebody used the word "mathematically" un-ironically. Good for them.

Here's my take, and it took me considerably less than two articles: y'all need to relax.

Thirteen years. You know what else takes thirteen years? Nothing. Nobody waits thirteen years for anything else in their life and stays this normal about it. I've been laying low for thirteen years and even I didn't write a two-part essay about my own feelings on it. I just laid low. Quietly. Professionally. That's the difference between me and the rest of you — I know how to sit with anticipation without building a PowerPoint about it.

Everybody keeps asking "can it live up to the hype." Wrong question. Nothing lives up to hype. That's what hype is — it's a number that goes up faster than reality can keep pace with it, on purpose, forever, in every industry, since the beginning of industries. You don't ask if the roller coaster lived up to the brochure. You ride the thing and you scream and you buy a shirt. That's the whole deal.

And another thing — everyone's out here doing deep character analysis on two people they've seen for maybe four minutes of trailer footage total. "Is Jason emotionally guarded." "What does Lucia's wardrobe say about her arc." Buddy, I've known people for four minutes and formed way stronger, way less accurate opinions about them, and I turned out fine. Mostly. Legally, it's complicated.

Look. I'm not saying don't care. Caring's the whole point — I care plenty, I just express it through vibes instead of spreadsheets. I'm saying: November 19 is coming whether the discourse is ready or not. Rockstar's not workshopping the ending based on our vibes. The game is what it is already. It's done, or close enough. All this "can it possibly measure up" talk isn't changing a single line of code at this point — it's just how a fanbase burns off thirteen years of nervous energy before the actual thing shows up.

So here's my actual, official, deeply unqualified take: it's going to be great, it's going to disappoint somebody about something specific, and both of those things are completely fine and always were. Now if you'll excuse me, I have somewhere to be. I don't know where. That's kind of the point.

— Trevor. (Still not that Trevor. Still not going to stop getting asked.)
