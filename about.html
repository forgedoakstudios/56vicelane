A Place To Belong

Gaming communities are built like families. The good, the bad, and the not so pretty. But what they give you — what they have always given people — is a place to belong. That has never changed. Not in forty years of gaming. Not through every console generation, every controversy, every evolution of the medium. The community remains.

56ViceLane was built from that belief. Not as a media company. Not as a brand. As a fan who has been in this community since the beginning, who has seen everything it can be, and who wanted to give it something real before the biggest transition in gaming history.

Who Built This

Christopher Brewster. Glasgow, Kentucky. Known to everyone as Brew — gamertag Musashi13 on PlayStation, tankerunknown on PC and Discord. Former competitive gamer from the GameBattles era. Country music songwriter performing as Boone Crockett. In gaming since the 1980s.

The ideas behind 56ViceLane have been building for thirteen years. Just a community that deserved a proper sendoff and the conviction to build it.

The Last Drive

On November 18, 2026 — the night before GTA6 launches — 56ViceLane is organizing The Last Drive. Players all over the world logging into GTA5 together for one final slow cruise through the city. No missions. No chaos. Just the road and the people you ran with.

Free. All platforms. Regional lobbies worldwide. Sign up below to get event details when they drop.

The Music

An original song written specifically for The Last Drive is coming. Three mixes — Country, Rock, and Pop — recorded under the Boone Crockett name. Written for the community. For the farewell. For everyone who ever called Los Santos home. Join the list to hear it first.
