Man Rescued From Billboard After Climbing Up To ‘Fix The Spelling,’ Which Was Not Actually Wrong

@LSOddities·Filed under: things that definitely happened

Fire crews responded just after midnight to reports of a man stranded roughly forty feet up the support ladder of a billboard advertising a local mattress outlet, having climbed up, in his words, "to fix the apostrophe situation."

The billboard reads: "YOUR NEW MATTRESS IS WAITING." There is no apostrophe on the billboard. There was never an apostrophe on the billboard. The man maintains, even now, even after the ladder truck arrived, even after the whole thing was over, that there absolutely should be one, and that this is "a hill he's willing to die on," a phrase he chose to say while forty feet in the air, which the responding captain called "not ideal messaging."

He was talked down over the course of about twenty-five minutes, during which he continued to explain, at volume, the proper use of the possessive apostrophe to a growing crowd of commuters who had stopped their cars specifically to watch this happen.

No charges have been filed. The mattress company has declined to comment on the grammar allegations but has confirmed the sign will not be changed.

The man was last seen accepting a blanket from paramedics and telling them, unprompted, that "it's ‘fewer,’ not ‘less,’ when you can count it," which nobody asked him.

— Filed by a fire crew that had a very long conversation about apostrophes
