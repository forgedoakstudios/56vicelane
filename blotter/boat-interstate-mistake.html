Man Steals Boat, Drives It Six Miles Down The Interstate Before Realizing His Mistake

@TrafficWatchLS·Filed under: things that definitely happened

At 2:14 this morning, Vice PD received seventeen separate calls reporting a twenty-two-foot center-console fishing boat traveling at a steady fifty-one miles per hour in the center lane of the interstate, running lights on, trolling motor down, no trailer, no visible means of propulsion beyond what investigators describe as "pure conviction."

The driver, later identified only as "a guy," told responding officers he had taken the boat from a marina because it was, in his words, "just sitting there, disrespectfully." When asked how he expected the boat to move on dry land, he reportedly paused for a long time and said, "I didn't think that far ahead, no."

Witnesses say the boat maintained highway speed for approximately six miles, navigated two exits without signaling, and came to a stop only when the man ran out of what he called "boat gas," which does not exist, and what investigators believe was simply momentum.

He was found sitting in the captain's chair, hazards on, waiting for what he described as "someone official" to explain what happens next. Someone official arrived. It did not go well for him. It went great for everyone else, who now have this.

The boat has been returned to its owner. The owner has requested it never be spoken of again. Vice City will not honor that request.
