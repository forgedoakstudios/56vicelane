Downtown High-Rise Reports Break-In; Only Item Missing Is A Single Golden Toilet

@LSCrimeDesk·Filed under: things that definitely happened

A break-in at a downtown penthouse left the unit almost entirely untouched — cash, art, and a safe containing what the owner describes only as "documents" were all left in place — except for one item: a fully functioning, solid gold toilet, professionally disconnected from the plumbing and removed without so much as a scuff on the tile.

The owner, who has requested to remain unnamed for reasons he described as "optics," says the toilet was worth "a significant amount of money, don't print the number," and that its removal must have taken "real professionals, plumbing professionals, this was not amateur hour."

Detectives note the thieves left behind two other bathrooms, both with regular, non-gold toilets, entirely undisturbed, suggesting this was not a general theft but a targeted operation with, in the words of one officer, "unbelievably specific priorities."

No suspects are in custody. Investigators are, however, asking anyone with information about the sudden appearance of a residential-grade gold toilet anywhere in the tri-county area to come forward, no questions asked about why you'd recognize one on sight.

The owner has already ordered a replacement. It is, we are told, "a little bigger this time."
