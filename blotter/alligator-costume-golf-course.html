Golf Course Evacuated After Man In Alligator Costume Reportedly ‘Just Wanted To Play A Round’

@LSOddities·Filed under: things that definitely happened

Emerald Isle Golf & Country Club was evacuated in full Tuesday morning after a man in a head-to-toe, professional-grade alligator costume was spotted approaching the third hole with a full set of clubs and, according to witnesses, "perfect etiquette."

Golfers fled. Groundskeepers fled. A wedding party using the clubhouse for photos fled, still in formalwear, and honestly got some great candid shots out of it. The man in the costume did not flee. The man in the costume waited patiently at the tee box for the group ahead of him to clear the green.

When confronted by course security, the man reportedly removed his mask just far enough to say, "I have a 10:40 tee time," and put the mask back on. He did, in fact, have a 10:40 tee time. It was under his real name. He had paid in full. Nothing in club policy prohibits alligator costumes on the course, a fact the club's lawyers have since confirmed and deeply regret confirming.

He was escorted off the property before completing his round. Course management has since added a section to the dress code. It is four pages long. It did not need to be four pages long. It is four pages long because of him.

He has not been identified. He has, however, already requested his tee time back for next Tuesday.

— Filed by the pro shop attendant who saw the whole thing and still isn't right
